# Concepts 4 — Narrative Engine Bank (31 Engines × 30 Seeds)

A fourth concept bank for the **story-photo-full-v1** pipeline, organized by **narrative engine** (the emotional machine that drives the story) instead of by setting (concepts1/3) or recognition mechanic (concepts2).

- **Why engines:** concepts1–3 almost all run on one engine — *humiliation → reveal*. This bank adds **30 other engines** so a batch can vary its emotional register and not feel one-note.
- **930 seeds** total: 31 engines × 30 seeds.
- Each seed = one line: **the core tension → the payoff**. Use as a Phase 0 seed, then build the 4-beat arc + Character Lock.
- **Mix freely** with any setting from `concepts3.md` and any recognition mechanic from `concepts2.md`.
- US settings, fictional characters, Google-policy safe.

**Source tags:** `concepts4 / Engine 07` or `concepts4 #E07-12`.

---

## How engines change the craft

The pipeline assumes *humiliation → reveal* by default. For these engines, adapt three things (see the methodology files — they now carry the engine-aware branches):

| Element | Default (humiliation) | Other engines |
|---|---|---|
| **Tension (Acts 1–2)** | unfairness, mockery | mystery, need, longing, a hard choice, a ticking clock |
| **Turn (Act 3)** | hidden identity surfaces | an action, a recognition, a choice, a return, a confession |
| **Payoff (Act 4)** | antagonist consequence | reward, reunion, vindication-by-result, poignancy, grace |

### Tags used below

- **Tone:** `feel-good`, `bittersweet`, or `mixed` (works either way depending on ending).
- **Photo rule:**
  - `twist-hidden` (default) — the photo shows the tension, never the payoff.
  - `action-in-photo` — for a small set of engines the **peak action *is* the photo** (the moment of rescue, of returning the wallet, of the deed being witnessed, of crossing the finish line). The *outcome/consequence* still stays out of frame. **Allowed only for: Crisis Hero (E05), The Honest Return (E07), Good Deed Witnessed (E19), Underdog Wins on Merit (E22).**

## Engine 01 — Kindness Rewarded / Pay-It-Forward

- **Emotional core:** a small, no-strings kindness returns multiplied. No villain required.
- **Tone:** feel-good · **Photo rule:** twist-hidden
- **Turn → Payoff:** the helped stranger returns / the kindness loops back → unexpected reward, often years later.

1. **The Paid-For Coffee**: A barista quietly covers a stranded traveler's bill → a year later he returns holding the keys to the café he just bought to save it from closing.
2. **The Bus-Fare Stranger**: A teen pays an old man's fare without a word → the man turns out to be the scholarship trustee who later signs her tuition.
3. **The Extra Sandwich**: A deli worker slips a free meal to a shivering job-seeker → he comes back as the manager who insists on hiring her.
4. **The Borrowed Umbrella**: A clerk hands her umbrella to a drenched mother → years later that mother, now a surgeon, recognizes her in a waiting room and waives the fee.
5. **The Late-Night Tow**: A mechanic fixes a flat for free at midnight → the driver returns to award his shop the city's whole fleet contract.
6. **The Spare Coat**: A commuter gives his coat to a man on a freezing platform → the man, a hiring director, remembers the face across an interview table.
7. **The Quiet Grocery Cover**: A shopper pays for a struggling dad's cart → that dad later becomes the loan officer who approves her first home.
8. **The Held Elevator**: An intern holds the elevator for a flustered visitor → the visitor is the board chair who later requests her by name.
9. **The Free Haircut**: A barber trims a homeless vet for free before an interview → the vet returns in uniform to repay it tenfold.
10. **The Shared Lunch**: A girl splits her lunch with a new kid no one likes → decades later he funds her failing clinic, remembering that day.
11. **The Returned Cart**: A worker walks a lost senior to her car in the rain → the senior leaves the store's underdog clerk a life-changing gift.
12. **The Quiet Tip**: A waitress comps a crying single mom's meal → the mom returns years later, now wealthy, to make the diner her foundation's partner.
13. **The Dropped Groceries**: A boy helps an old woman gather spilled groceries → she turns out to be the principal who later fights for his scholarship.
14. **The Open Door**: A janitor lets a soaked stranger wait inside after hours → the stranger, a developer, later saves the building from demolition.
15. **The Bag of Books**: A librarian gifts a poor student the books she can't buy → the student returns as the donor who rebuilds the library.
16. **The Free Repair**: A cobbler mends a child's only shoes for nothing → that child grows up to anonymously fund the whole block's small shops.
17. **The Shared Charger**: A traveler lends her phone charger to a panicked stranger → he's the recruiter who later remembers her kindness over her résumé.
18. **The Covered Prescription**: A pharmacy tech quietly pays a senior's copay → the senior's son, a hospital director, later hires her on the spot.
19. **The Warm Meal**: A cook sends a plate out to a busker in the cold → the busker, now signed, dedicates his first hit to "the kitchen that fed me."
20. **The Free Ride Home**: A driver waives the fare for a stranded nurse after a double shift → she's the one who later saves his daughter in the ER.
21. **The Spare Ticket**: A fan gives his last game ticket to a fatherless kid → the kid, now a coach, returns to give the man's grandson a tryout.
22. **The Quiet Babysit**: A neighbor watches a single mom's kids free for a week → that mom becomes the lawyer who later defends her for free.
23. **The Paid Layaway**: A stranger clears a struggling family's layaway before Christmas → the father returns years later to do the same for a hundred families in her name.
24. **The Held Seat**: A passenger gives up first class for an exhausted soldier → the soldier's general later writes the letter that changes the passenger's career.
25. **The Lent Tools**: A handyman lends his only tools to a desperate neighbor → the neighbor's startup, built with them, later employs the whole street.
26. **The Free Tutoring**: A teacher coaches a failing kid after hours for nothing → the kid, now a CEO, endows her department decades later.
27. **The Covered Tab**: A regular pays the bill of a laid-off stranger at the bar → the stranger, rehired and risen, returns to buy the bar before it shutters.
28. **The Shared Cab**: A woman splits her cab with a stranger in the rain → he's the editor who later remembers her name on a submission pile.
29. **The Quiet Donation**: A clerk drops her own cash into a child's short register total → the child's mother repays it years later as the bank manager who saves her home.
30. **The Open Garage**: A retiree shelters a stranded family in his garage overnight → their son returns decades later to rebuild the man's collapsing house for free.

## Engine 02 — Hidden Sacrifice Revealed

12. **The Quiet Landlord**: Tenants assume he's just forgetful about rent → he's been quietly forgiving payments for families in trouble for years.
13. **The Librarian's Salary**: Staff wonder why she never upgrades anything → her checks have funded a child literacy program no one knew existed.
14. **The Empty Chair at Dinner**: A son resents his mother's long absences → she spent those nights caring for a dying stranger no family claimed.

19. **The Worn Wedding Ring**: A widow is thought too poor to replace it → she pawned everything else to keep a scholarship in her husband's name alive.

## Engine 07 — The Honest Return  ·  [ACTION-IN-PHOTO ALLOWED]

- **Emotional core:** integrity under hardship — returning what isn't yours even when you have nothing.
- **Tone:** feel-good · **Photo rule:** **action-in-photo** (capture the moment of handing it back; the *reward* stays out of frame).
- **Turn → Payoff:** the poor/struggling finder returns the valuables → an unexpected reward, job, or recognition follows.

1. **The Found Wallet**: A homeless man returns a fat wallet to a banker → the banker, shamed by his own assumption, offers him a job.
2. **The Cash on the Curb**: A struggling single mom turns in an envelope of cash she found → the owner sets up a fund in her name.

## 4-beat arcs + Character Lock

### C28001 — F01-17 | Relatives Fight Over "Worthless" Paintings → One Canvas Is a Missing Master

- **Setup:** After patriarch Hank Ellison dies, his five adult children gather with the estate lawyer to divide his art collection — a pile of dusty canvases everyone assumes are amateur work.
- **Clue:** Youngest daughter Nora, the only one who visited Hank weekly, notices a small signature and gallery stamp on the back of one canvas — a detail her siblings have ignored.
- **Turn:** An independent appraiser Nora quietly hired enters the office mid-argument and identifies the canvas as a missing Impressionist study worth more than the rest of the estate combined.
- **Payoff:** The siblings who mocked Nora's "sentimental attachment" to the paintings are stunned; Nora inherits the collection per a clause naming the child who "valued the art, not the assets."

**Character Lock:**
- Nora Ellison — 31, youngest daughter, cream cable-knit sweater, tortoiseshell glasses, brown ponytail, holding a manila envelope against her chest
- Graham Ellison — 44, eldest brother, navy sport coat, gold signet ring, arms crossed dismissively
- Louise Yun — 55, estate appraiser (independent), charcoal blazer, leather portfolio, calm authority
- Setting — Estate lawyer's office in Charleston, South Carolina; mahogany conference table, six dusty framed canvases leaning against the wall, afternoon light through plantation shutters
- Object clue — a small gallery stamp on the back of one canvas frame, partially visible where it leans (pays off when appraiser identifies it)

**Photo rule:** twist-hidden
**Opening style:** Cold-open dialogue

### C28002 — F04-03 | Brother Finishes Glowing Eulogy → The "Dead" Man Clears His Throat in the Back Pew

- **Setup:** Derek Voss delivers a polished eulogy at his brother Ray's funeral, praising Ray's generosity while privately celebrating that the life insurance payout will clear his debts.
- **Clue:** A figure in the back pew wears a familiar corduroy jacket; mourners assume he is a distant relative.
- **Turn:** Ray Voss stands from the back pew and clears his throat — he faked his death with a hospice nurse's help after discovering Derek forged beneficiary documents.
- **Payoff:** Derek's insurance fraud is exposed to the room full of witnesses; the payout he counted on becomes evidence against him.

**Character Lock:**
- Derek Voss — 47, older brother, black suit and silver tie, reading glasses perched on nose, eulogy cards in hand, composure cracking
- Ray Voss — 42, younger brother, alive, tan corduroy jacket over a flannel shirt, three-day stubble, standing slowly from back pew
- Pastor Linda Greer — 60, white robe, standing beside the lectern, hand on her chest in shock
- Setting — Grace Memorial Chapel, Savannah, Georgia; dark wood pews, lilies on an empty casket, stained glass window casting amber light, Tuesday 11am
- Object clue — the empty casket with a closed lid and lilies draped over it (pays off when Ray is revealed alive)

### C28008 — F20-09 | Neighbor Moves Fence Overnight → Survey Restores Line and Oil Pad Was on Widow's Side

- **Setup:** After her husband Earl's death, widow Connie Raines wakes to find her neighbor Bud Haskell has moved the fence line twelve feet onto her pasture — right over the spot where an oil company wants to drill.
- **Clue:** Connie's grandson, Mateo, a surveying student, arrives with a transit and the original 1952 plat from the county records.
- **Turn:** The survey proves the fence was moved; worse for Bud, the oil pad access point sits entirely on Connie's original parcel. Bud bet on a grieving widow not fighting back.
- **Payoff:** Connie files the corrected plat, the oil lease names her as sole mineral-rights holder, and Bud's bluff collapses. The "test" he set — assuming she was powerless — fails because she raised a grandson who knows the land.

**Character Lock:**
- Connie Raines — 71, widow, sun-weathered face, straw hat, denim work shirt, arms crossed at the fence, resolute
- Bud Haskell — 62, neighbor rancher, sweat-stained ball cap, plaid pearl-snap shirt, standing behind the freshly moved fence posts with a shovel
- Mateo Raines — 24, grandson, surveying student, orange safety vest, holding a transit level on a tripod, plat map rolled under his arm
- Setting — Mesquite fence line between two ranches, Midland, Texas; flat scrubland, pump jack silhouette in distance, golden late-afternoon light, red dirt
- Object clue — the freshly dug fence posts with still-wet concrete (proving the move was recent)

### C28010 — F29-20 | Aftercare Lottery Rigged for Friends → Statistician Parent Reproduces Non-Random Draw

- **Setup:** At Westbrook Elementary, PTA president Gail Fenton runs the aftercare lottery "fairly" — but the same twelve families always get slots while others are waitlisted year after year.
- **Clue:** Parent Deepa Rao, a biostatistician, runs the last five years of lottery outcomes through a chi-squared test and finds the probability of this distribution occurring randomly is less than one in ten thousand.
- **Turn:** Deepa presents her analysis at the open board meeting; the "random" lottery used a seeded algorithm Gail's husband wrote, prioritizing board members' children.
- **Payoff:** Bittersweet — the lottery is reformed, Deepa's daughter finally gets a slot, but the damage of years of exclusion for working parents who scrambled for childcare can't be undone.

**Character Lock:**
- Deepa Rao — 38, biostatistician parent, reading glasses, navy cardigan over a print blouse, holding a printed spreadsheet and a laptop
- Gail Fenton — 45, PTA president, blonde blowout, monogrammed tote, standing at a podium with a forced smile
- Setting — Westbrook Elementary cafeteria, Tempe, Arizona; folding chairs in rows, a projector screen behind the podium, fluorescent lights, Tuesday evening 7pm
- Object clue — Deepa's printed spreadsheet with highlighted rows (held face-out toward the audience but text not readable at photo distance)

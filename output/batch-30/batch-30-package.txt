I think about the parade sometimes. The moment before I walked to the microphone. The thirty seconds of decision. My press badge and my trench coat and the folded printout in my hand and the choice — stay in the press section where reporters belong, or walk to the stage where the truth needed to be.

I chose the stage. Not because I am brave. Because Maria Delgado was on the curb. Because James Fitch was by the hot dog cart. Because real service was standing on the sidewalk watching a lie get a key to the city, and someone had to say it out loud.

That is the job. Not the story. Not the byline. Not the clicks. The job is putting the truth in the air where people can hear it and letting them decide what to do.

They decided well. They always do, when they have the facts.

I am Mia Cartwright. Forty-one. Reporter. Press badge and a printout.

And the real heroes were never on the float.

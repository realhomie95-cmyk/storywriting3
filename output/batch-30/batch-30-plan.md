## 4-beat arcs + Character Lock

### C30001 — F02-10 | CEO Dismisses Janitor's Claim → DNA Proves Janitor Is His Twin Separated at Birth

- **Setup:** CEO Harrison Wyatt dismisses maintenance worker Darnell Price's claim that they share a mother, calling it a scam at a company all-hands.
- **Clue:** Darnell holds a sealed envelope from a family court-ordered ancestry test — the judge mandated it after Darnell's birth mother petitioned for reunion.
- **Turn:** The court officer opens the results live: Darnell and Harrison share identical markers — they are monozygotic twins separated at birth by a closed adoption.
- **Payoff:** Harrison's dismissal becomes public humiliation as the room witnesses his cruelty toward his own brother; Darnell inherits standing in the family estate.

**Character Lock:**
- Darnell Price — 38, maintenance worker, dark brown skin, close fade haircut, gray janitorial polo with a name patch, holding a sealed manila envelope flat against his thigh
- Harrison Wyatt — 38, CEO, same facial structure as Darnell, tailored charcoal suit, platinum cufflinks, standing at a lectern with arms wide in dismissal
- Judge Alma Reeves — 62, black robe, reading glasses on a chain, seated behind a raised bench with a manila folder open
- Setting — Family court, Denver, Colorado; fluorescent-lit courtroom, blond wood bench, gallery with a few observers, Tuesday 10am
- Object clue — the sealed manila envelope in Darnell's hand (DNA results, pays off when opened)

**Photo rule:** twist-hidden
**Opening style:** Direct address

### C30002 — F05-18 | Daughter Opens Family Calendar → Recurring "Client Dinners" Map to One Apartment

- **Setup:** Sixteen-year-old Mara Calloway opens the shared family calendar to schedule a dentist appointment and notices her father Owen has recurring Thursday "client dinners" at the same address every week.
- **Clue:** She maps the address — it is a residential apartment, not a restaurant. Her mother Lena is beside her, looking over her shoulder.
- **Turn:** Lena cross-references the address with the name on the lease — it belongs to Owen's office assistant, the one he said he "let go" months ago.
- **Payoff:** Owen comes home to an empty house; Lena has taken Mara to her sister's. His own calendar, shared for "family transparency," became the evidence that undid him.

**Character Lock:**
- Lena Calloway — 42, mother, ash-blonde hair in a messy clip, oversized cardigan over pajama pants, standing behind Mara's desk chair, one hand covering her mouth
- Mara Calloway — 16, daughter, dark hair in a ponytail, school hoodie, seated at the desk, pointing at the computer screen with wide eyes
- Setting — Calloway home office/den, Nashville, Tennessee; small corner desk with a desktop monitor showing a calendar grid, bulletin board with family photos, warm desk lamp, Thursday evening 8pm
- Object clue — the desktop monitor displaying a repeating calendar event on Thursdays (colored block visible, text not readable)

- **Payoff:** Bittersweet — Gemma confesses, Reid says he'd have swiped right on her too, but trust has to be rebuilt from scratch. They start over — as themselves.

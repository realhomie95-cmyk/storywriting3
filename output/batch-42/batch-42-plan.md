### C42001 — F01-08 | Nephew Celebrates Inheritance → Janitor He Fired Holds Veto Power

**Seed:** F01-08 — A nephew is told he inherits the company → the trust protector arrives — the janitor he fired — with power to freeze every share

**Engine:** E00 — Humiliation → Reveal | **Tone:** mixed | **Opening:** Dialogue Slap

**Arc:**
1. **Setup:** At the Bellamy & Associates law office in Savannah, GA, nephew Craig Bellamy, 34, is told by estate attorney Dorothy Farris that he inherits 100% of the family textile company after his grandfather's passing.
2. **Clue:** Craig is already listing people to fire. He jokes about "cleaning house" — starting with the janitor he terminated last month, Hosea Grant, 61.
3. **Turn:** Dorothy opens the trust addendum: a trust protector was named six months before death. The protector has veto authority over all share transfers and board votes. The protector is Hosea Grant — who enters the conference room with the signed certification.
4. **Payoff:** Hosea freezes Craig's share transfers, requires a culture audit before any layoffs, and reinstates three workers Craig fired. Craig inherits the title but not the power.

**Character Lock:**
- CRAIG BELLAMY — 34, white, gelled brown hair, fitted navy blazer over open-collar shirt, Rolex, smirk; entitled grandson, already planning "cleaning house"
- HOSEA GRANT — 61, Black, broad-shouldered, salt-and-pepper close-cropped hair, pressed khaki work shirt, steel-toe boots; former janitor, quiet dignity, holds trust protector certificate
- DOROTHY FARRIS — 58, white, silver bob, reading glasses, charcoal blazer; estate attorney, neutral
- Setting: Bellamy & Associates conference room, Bull Street, Savannah, GA; Thursday 2 PM, afternoon sun through plantation shutters
- Object clue: trust protector certification document in Hosea's hand

**Peak-tension (photo):** Craig leaning back in his chair grinning while Dorothy reads the will aloud, Hosea visible through the glass conference door approaching with a folder — Beat 2, twist NOT shown.

**Character Lock:**

### C42008 — F20-06 | Pipeline Company Threatens Eminent Domain → Widow Owns the Water Rights

**Seed:** F20-06 — Company threatens eminent domain → easement map crosses protected aquifer — widow owns water rights

**Engine:** E09 — Slow-Burn Devotion | **Tone:** feel-good | **Opening:** Overheard line

**Arc:**
1. **Setup:** In Midland, TX, Texana Pipeline Corp sends landman Dale Hubbard, 44, to pressure widow Marisol Fuentes, 72, into signing a pipeline easement across her 320-acre ranch. The company offers $12,000 — a fraction of value — and threatens eminent domain if she refuses.
2. **Clue:** Marisol's late husband, Esteban, was a hydrogeologist. Before he died, he filed water rights on the Ogallala Aquifer section beneath their land — a protected zone. The pipeline route crosses directly through it. Marisol keeps his filed paperwork in a cedar chest.
3. **Turn:** At a county commission hearing, Dale presents the pipeline route. Marisol stands with her husband's filed water rights certificate and an EPA aquifer protection map. The route is illegal without her consent — and eminent domain cannot override federal aquifer protections.
4. **Payoff:** The commission denies Texana's easement application. Marisol's water rights — Esteban's final gift — protect the ranch and twenty neighboring farms. Dale's company offers $1.2M for a reroute. Marisol declines: "My husband put this in the ground for everyone. It stays."

**Character Lock:**
- MARISOL FUENTES — 72, Mexican-American, silver braid, weathered sun hat, denim work shirt, turquoise ring; rancher widow, quiet strength, honors Esteban's legacy
- DALE HUBBARD — 44, white, pressed western shirt, bolo tie, cowboy boots, leather portfolio; landman, aggressive, dismissive of "an old woman"

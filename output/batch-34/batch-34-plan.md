### C34001 | F01-17 | Relatives Fight Over "Worthless" Paintings → One Canvas Is a Missing Master

**Seed:** Siblings fight over worthless furniture → hidden compartment holds stock certificates (adapted: Relatives fight over worthless paintings → appraiser identifies one canvas as missing master worth the entire estate)

**Engine:** E12 — Karma / Comeuppance

**Arc:**
1. **Setup:** Three siblings gather in a Savannah estate law office for their late father's reading. Brothers Vernon and Dale claim the house, cars, and accounts. They shove the "junk art" to their quiet sister Nadine — twenty-two paintings their father kept in the attic.
2. **Clue:** Nadine notices a small brass plate on the back of one frame — a registry number she's seen in art history books. She photographs it silently while Vernon signs transfer papers.
3. **Turn:** Two weeks later, at the finalization meeting, an appraiser from Sotheby's confirms canvas #14 is a lost Winslow Homer seascape — authenticated, provenance-matched, estimated at $4.7 million. The "worthless junk" Nadine received is worth more than the entire remaining estate combined.
4. **Payoff:** Vernon and Dale's greed gave Nadine the fortune. The will's residual clause states whichever heir holds the art collection also inherits the father's intellectual property rights — including royalties from his engineering patents. Nadine donates one painting to the Telfair Museum in their father's name. The brothers get the house with the leaking roof.

**Character Lock:**
- NADINE OSEI — 37, Black, natural TWA haircut, wire-rim glasses, olive linen blouse, quiet art history professor; patient, observant, never raises her voice
- VERNON OSEI — 44, Black, tailored gray suit, gold cufflinks, real estate developer; loud, dismissive, eldest son
- DALE OSEI — 41, Black, polo shirt, expensive watch, car dealership owner; follows Vernon's lead
- ARTHUR OSEI (deceased) — father, retired mechanical engineer, secret art collector
- Setting: Whitmore & Associates estate law office, Bull Street, Savannah, GA; Wednesday 2 PM, afternoon sun through plantation shutters
- Object clue: small brass plate with registry number on back of painting frame

**Peak-tension (photo):** Vernon pushes a dusty stack of framed canvases across a conference table toward Nadine while Dale smirks; Nadine's hand rests near the frames, her eyes steady and calm — Beat 2, twist NOT shown.

### C34002 | F04-03 | Brother Finishes Glowing Eulogy → The Subject Clears His Throat in the Back Pew

**Seed:** A brother finishes a glowing eulogy → the subject clears his throat in the back pew

**Engine:** E00 — Humiliation → Reveal

2. **Clue:** A mourner in the back pew shifts in his seat. He wears a worn denim jacket and a ball cap pulled low. The funeral director frowns — she didn't check this man in. His hands grip a folder.

**Character Lock:**
- PRESTON WHITMORE — 52, white, silver-streaked dark hair, black three-piece suit, pocket square; charming, performative grief, insurance fraudster
- GRAHAM WHITMORE — 48, white, gaunt, stubbled jaw, denim jacket over flannel, ball cap; recently discharged VA patient, quiet, determined

I didn't.

It took me forty minutes to find the first violation.

The fall zone around the climbing wall is undersized. State code requires a minimum of six feet of impact-attenuating surface in every direction from any elevated play element over thirty inches. The design shows four feet on the north side. Two feet under minimum.

Violation one.

The guardrails on the elevated platform are thirty-four inches high. Code requires thirty-eight inches minimum for platforms above forty-eight inches. The platform in this design is sixty inches.

Violation two.

The climbing wall is anchored to a concrete footing that measures twelve by twelve inches. For a structure of that height and load capacity, the minimum footing dimension per code is twenty-four by twenty-four.

Violation three.

Three violations. In thirty-seven pages. By a contractor who was awarded the job because his wife is PTA president.

## 4-beat arcs + Character Lock

### C29001 — F06-15 | Tenants Complain About Broken Machines → The Woman Folding Socks Owns the Block
- **Setup:** Angry tenants confront a quiet woman in the laundromat about broken washers, demanding she "tell the owner."
- **Clue:** She folds her socks calmly, a set of keys on a lanyard around her wrist — more keys than any tenant would carry.
- **Turn:** The building manager arrives and addresses her as the owner of the laundromat and twelve other properties on the street.
- **Payoff (story):** Tenants realize the woman they berated is their landlord; she announces repairs — and a rent review for those who were disrespectful.
- **Lock:** Dolores Fermin — 58, quiet Filipina woman, oversized gray cardigan, reading glasses on a chain, folding laundry at the center table, a heavy key lanyard on her wrist; Marcus Tate — 34, lead complainer, Carhartt jacket, pointing at a broken dryer; Jocelyn, building manager — 40, clipboard, entering from the street door. Setting: Suds & Spin laundromat, Flatbush, Brooklyn, NY, rainy Tuesday evening, fluorescent light, rows of front-loaders. Object clue: heavy key lanyard on Dolores's wrist.

### C29002 — F21-25 | Collector Demands All-In Payment → Debtor Slides a Deed Across the Felt
- **Setup:** Loan shark Victor Sava corners Nate Briggs at a back table on a casino floor, demanding full repayment tonight.
- **Clue:** Nate reaches into his jacket — not for money, but for a folded legal document.
- **Turn:** The document is a deed: Nate purchased the casino in a quiet acquisition. Victor's operation has been running inside Nate's property.
- **Payoff (story):** Security — now Nate's security — escorts Victor out. The predator operated on the prey's land all along.
- **Lock:** Nate Briggs — 37, calm, black henley under a worn leather jacket, short beard, a folded legal document in his inner pocket; Victor Sava — 52, loan shark, burgundy silk shirt, gold bracelet, leaning across a green felt table, index finger jabbing; Casino floor — The Pearl, Atlantic City, NJ, Saturday 11 PM, dim amber lighting, slot machine glow in background. Object clue: folded document half-visible from Nate's jacket pocket.

### C29003 — F04-03 | Brother Finishes the Eulogy → The Subject Clears His Throat in the Back Pew
- **Setup:** Emmett Landry delivers a tearful eulogy for his brother Joel at a Savannah church; mourners weep.
- **Clue:** A man in a rumpled coat slips into the last pew during the eulogy, head bowed — half hidden by a column.
- **Turn:** Joel is alive. He clears his throat and stands. He faked his death to escape people in this room who were embezzling his estate.
- **Payoff (story):** The misunderstanding explained: Joel reveals he went to the FBI, not to the grave. The real rift — years of silence — is reconciled with Emmett.
- **Lock:** Emmett Landry — 44, older brother, charcoal suit, red-rimmed eyes, gripping the podium; Joel Landry — 40, the "deceased," rumpled tan overcoat, unshaven, standing at the back pew; Diane Landry — 67, mother, front row, clutching a funeral program. Setting: First Baptist Church, Savannah, GA, Thursday 2 PM, stained glass light, mahogany pews. Object clue: Joel's tan overcoat (identical to one in the casket photo display).

### C29004 — F14-02 | Agency Lawyer Dismisses Her Questions → Clause 14 Gives Her Custody
- **Setup:** Surrogate mother Rae Chen asks questions about the contract at an agency meeting; the lawyer waves her off.
- **Clue:** Rae's finger rests on a highlighted line — Clause 14 — that no one else at the table has read carefully.
- **Turn:** If the intended parents miss a single escrow payment, custody reverts to the surrogate. They missed one last Friday.
- **Payoff (story):** Rae's quiet literacy of her own contract gives her standing. The agency scrambles; the intended parents must negotiate from below.
- **Lock:** Rae Chen — 31, surrogate, simple white blouse, dark hair in a clip, reading glasses, one finger on a highlighted contract line; Arthur Baines — 55, agency lawyer, pinstripe vest, leaning back with arms crossed; Intended parents (David & Megan Torres) — mid-30s, matching navy, nervous glances. Setting: Pinnacle Family Agency conference room, Scottsdale, AZ, Wednesday 10 AM, glass-walled room, desert light through blinds. Object clue: yellow-highlighted Clause 14 on the open contract.

### C29005 — F29-11 | PTA Incumbent Runs Unopposed → Write-In Candidate Wins by Landslide

Object clue: overflowing ballot box on the folding table. **Photo rule: action-in-photo** (E22 — the winning instant: hand-count announced, Simone's hand raised).

### C29006 — F02-24 | Billionaire Refuses Donor → Donor Is His Son, Conceived the Night He Paid Her to Leave
- **Setup:** Wealthy businessman Richard Voss needs a kidney. His team searches. A young man arrives as a match.

- **Payoff (story):** Bittersweet devotion — Caleb came not for revenge but to save a father he never met. Richard must reckon with the life he bought silence over.

### C29007 — F18-13 | CEO Wins Pitch on Stage → Removed Co-Founder Has the Prototype

- **Clue:** In the back row, a woman with a laptop bag sits still — Priya Madan, the co-founder he erased from the deck.

- **Payoff (story):** Full-circle reversal: Priya now holds the power but responds with grace — offers partnership terms, not revenge. Derek must accept or fail publicly.
- **Lock:** Derek Nash — 38, CEO, fitted navy blazer, open collar, wireless mic clipped, standing center stage with a slide behind him; Priya Madan — 34, removed co-founder, black turtleneck, laptop bag on her knee, seated last row; Judges panel — three silhouettes at a long table. Setting: Capital Factory accelerator stage, Austin, TX, Thursday 4 PM, stage lights and a projector screen. Object clue: Priya's closed laptop in her lap (prototype inside).

### C29009 — F20-01 | Neighbors Mock Widow's Dusty Field → Geologist Confirms Formation Under Her Land
- **Setup:** Ranch neighbors laugh at elderly widow Opal Birdsong's untouched parcel, calling it worthless scrub.
- **Clue:** A university geologist's truck is parked by her fence at dawn — Opal invited him months ago.
- **Turn:** Core samples confirm a producing shale formation directly beneath Opal's land. She has been the quiet provider for the community well fund all along.
- **Payoff (story):** Warm surprise — Opal's "worthless" land is the richest in the county. She offers neighbors shared water infrastructure, turning mockery into gratitude.
- **Lock:** Opal Birdsong — 74, widow, faded denim shirt, straw hat, leaning on a fence post, looking out at a dusty field; Hank Dobbs — 50, neighbor rancher, pearl-snap shirt, smirking from his truck window; Dr. Luis Reyna — 42, university geologist, khaki field vest, holding a core sample tube. Setting: Birdsong ranch fence line, Elk City, OK, dawn, orange sky over flat scrubland, a single rusted pumpjack in the distance. Object clue: core sample tube in geologist's hand.

### C29010 — F07-25 | Hotel GM Fires Maid for Snooping → She Plays Recording of Him Selling Guest Data
- **Setup:** General manager Paul Atherton fires housekeeper Luisa Vega on the spot, accusing her of rifling guest rooms.

- **Payoff (story):** Luisa is a corporate compliance investigator placed undercover. Paul is escorted out by police who were waiting in the parking lot.
- **Lock:** Luisa Vega — 33, undercover investigator posing as housekeeper, gray hotel uniform, dark ponytail, calm expression, hand near apron pocket; Paul Atherton — 46, GM, tailored charcoal suit, pointing at the lobby door, face flushed with authority; Front desk staff — frozen mid-task. Setting: The Lark Hotel lobby, Denver, CO, Thursday 6 PM, polished concrete floors, brass fixtures, soft lobby music. Object clue: Luisa's hand resting on the apron pocket (phone/recorder inside).

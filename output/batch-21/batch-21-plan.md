### C231 | Mocked Janitor → Majority Shareholder

**Seed:** F06-01 — Executives mock the night janitor mopping during their layoff meeting → he removes his badge revealing he holds sixty percent of the company shares

**Engine:** E00 — Humiliation → Reveal

**Arc:**
1. **Setup:** Marcus Webb, 58, night janitor at Calloway-Briggs Financial, mops the 14th-floor boardroom while five executives run a midnight layoff session. They joke about adding him to the list.
2. **Clue:** CFO Danielle Hargrove tosses a crumpled spreadsheet at his mop bucket. Marcus picks it up calmly — the sheet shows the company's debt-to-equity ratio he already knows by heart.
3. **Turn:** Security is called to escort Marcus out. He removes his badge lanyard — underneath is a second badge: "Webb Capital Holdings, Managing Director." He holds 60% of voting shares through a blind trust.
4. **Payoff:** Marcus cancels the layoffs, dissolves Danielle's position, and promotes the mailroom clerk she bullied into a VP seat. The building was his all along.

**Character Lock:**
- MARCUS WEBB — 58, Black, gray-templed, lean build, wears navy coveralls and steel-toe boots; quiet dignity, never raises voice; US Marine veteran, self-made investor
- DANIELLE HARGROVE — 44, white, blond bob, charcoal Chanel blazer, pearl earrings; CFO, condescending, tosses paper at Marcus
- Setting: Calloway-Briggs Financial HQ, 14th floor corner boardroom, downtown Atlanta, GA; midnight session, city lights through floor-to-ceiling glass
- Object clue: crumpled spreadsheet in mop bucket; second badge hidden under lanyard

**Peak-tension (photo):** Marcus mopping under fluorescent lights, Danielle laughing mid-toss of crumpled paper toward his bucket, other executives smirking — Beat 2, twist NOT shown.

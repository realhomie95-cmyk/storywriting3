### C32001 — F01-17 | Relatives Fight Over Worthless Paintings → Appraiser Identifies a Missing Master Worth the Entire Estate
- **Setup:** At a contentious estate reading in the family library, greedy nephew Craig Whitmore sneers at his aunt Lorene's "junk paintings" she inherited — demanding the real estate goes to him.
- **Clue:** One canvas behind the stack shows unusual brushwork and a hidden signature beneath the varnish.
- **Turn:** The appraiser brought by Craig to prove the art worthless freezes mid-valuation — one painting is a documented missing Sargent watercolor worth $4.2 million, more than the rest of the estate combined.
- **Payoff (story):** Craig's demand to "take the real stuff" leaves him with the house and car; Lorene's "junk" inheritance makes her wealthier than the family has been in three generations. His cruelty looped back.
- **Lock:** Lorene Whitmore 64 (aunt, linen blouse, reading glasses on a chain, calm hands resting on stack of framed canvases, quiet confidence); Craig Whitmore 38 (nephew, slim-fit suit, loafers without socks, smirking with arms crossed, glass of bourbon in one hand); Appraiser Margot Chen 50 (pencil skirt, magnifying loupe around neck, holding canvas up to window light, expression shifting from routine to shock). Setting: family estate library, Austin, TX, Thursday 2pm, floor-to-ceiling bookshelves, Persian rug, dusty oil paintings leaning against the wall, afternoon sun through plantation shutters. Object clue: one canvas tilted toward the window with unusual color depth visible.

### C32002 — F05-09 | Wife Finds Husband's Group Chat on Unlocked Phone → The Chat Exposes His Coworker Organizing a Surprise Anniversary Trip for Her
- **Setup:** Megan Holt sees her husband Drew's phone buzz on the kitchen counter — a group chat notification with a thread titled with their home address makes her stomach drop.
- **Clue:** The chat name looks damning, but the messages inside are coworkers coordinating flights, a hotel reservation, and the words "she has no idea — 10 years deserves something big."
- **Turn:** Megan reads further — Drew's best friend, his sister, and three coworkers have been planning a surprise anniversary trip to the coast for weeks. The secrecy was love, not betrayal.
- **Payoff (story):** Megan's relief crashes into gratitude; Drew finds her crying in the kitchen and she confesses she saw everything. He laughs, holds her, says "you were never supposed to see it until Friday." The kindness of everyone involved rewards her years of being the family anchor.
- **Lock:** Megan Holt 36 (wife, oversized sleep shirt, bare feet, standing at kitchen island, phone in hand, expression cycling from dread to confusion to tears); Drew Holt 38 (husband, not yet present — enters later in running shorts and T-shirt, sweaty from morning jog, concerned then laughing); Kitchen setting detail: granite island, morning light, coffee maker mid-brew. Setting: suburban kitchen, Portland, OR, Saturday 7:15am, rain-streaked window above the sink, hanging copper pots. Object clue: phone screen glowing on the counter (text not readable to camera).

### C32003 — F07-15 | Family Complains About New Aide's Accent → Aide Is a State Inspector Documenting Neglect
- **Setup:** At Sunrise Memory Care in Tampa, the Brennan family complains loudly to the director about the new aide assigned to their mother — too slow, "barely speaks English," beneath their standard.

### C32004 — F10-04 | HR Insists Meeting Was Not Recorded → Employee Shares the Cloud Link Marked Auto-Record
- **Setup:** Software engineer Anika Desai was terminated in a Zoom meeting where the HR director and VP made discriminatory remarks about her maternity leave. When she filed a complaint, HR claimed no recording existed.
- **Clue:** The company's own Zoom enterprise account has auto-record enabled for all meetings by IT policy — a policy HR forgot about or hoped no one would check.
- **Turn:** Anika's attorney subpoenas the cloud storage. The recording exists — 47 minutes of timestamped audio with the VP saying "we can't have people disappearing for months and expecting their seat back" and the HR director laughing.
- **Payoff (story):** The recording becomes Exhibit A. Anika's wrongful termination suit results in reinstatement, back pay, and a policy overhaul. The VP is terminated. HR director is reassigned. Anika stood alone — and the system's own technology proved her right.
- **Lock:** Anika Desai 31 (software engineer, blazer over a nursing tank, laptop open, composed fury in her jaw, printed Zoom transcript in a folder on the table); HR Director Bill Stokes 52 (dress shirt rolled to elbows, leaning back in chair, dismissive half-smile); VP of Engineering Mark Callahan 46 (gray quarter-zip, standing near the whiteboard, arms crossed); Attorney Sandra Obi 40 (Anika's lawyer, dark suit, standing behind Anika's chair, one hand on the folder). Setting: HR conference room, tech company floor, Manhattan, NY, Wednesday 3pm, glass-walled room, whiteboard with org chart, city skyline through floor-to-ceiling windows behind. Object clue: laptop screen showing Zoom cloud recording icon (small cloud symbol, not readable text).

### C32005 — F14-21 | Parents Rush to File Birth Certificate → Surrogate Filed First, Naming Herself Legal Mother
- **Setup:** Intended parents Cameron and Tess Archer race to the county registrar to file their newborn's birth certificate — only to learn that the surrogate, Julie Medina, filed four hours earlier, listing herself as the biological and legal mother.
- **Clue:** Julie's filing wasn't malicious — she discovered through the hospital's genetic screening that the embryo transferred was hers, not the Archers'. The clinic made the swap. Julie is the biological mother.
- **Turn:** Julie meets the Archers at the registrar's office not as an adversary but as a protector — she filed first to prevent the clinic from covering up their error. She hands them the genetic report and says, "I'm not taking your baby. I'm making sure you know the truth before anyone buries it."
- **Payoff (story):** Julie's selfless filing protects both families legally. The clinic's malpractice is exposed. The Archers and Julie work out a guardianship that honors biology and intention both — because Julie became the provider in disguise, shielding them from a cover-up.
- **Lock:** Cameron Archer 34 (intended father, denim jacket, holding infant car seat, bewildered); Tess Archer 33 (intended mother, postpartum exhaustion visible, cardigan over hospital discharge clothes, hands gripping a manila folder); Julie Medina 29 (surrogate/biological mother, flannel shirt, jeans, calm and resolved, genetic report printout in hand); Registrar clerk Donna 58 (behind counter, glasses on a chain, form in hand, uncertain expression). - **Payoff (story):** The landman's lowball is exposed; Opal retains her minerals and negotiates a royalty lease on her own terms. The family that was being exploited becomes the family that dictates terms.
- **Lock:** Opal Jeffers 74 (widow, floral housedress, gardening gloves tucked in pocket, wire-rim glasses, standing on her porch with one hand on the railing, skeptical squint); Landman Rick Sumner 42 (khaki pants, company polo, clipboard, pen extended toward Opal, salesman smile faltering); Elijah Jeffers 27 (grandson, cargo shorts, Georgia Tech T-shirt, rolled geological survey map in hand, confident stride up porch steps). ### C32008 — F24-09 | Wrong Twin Appears for Sentencing → Victim Stands and Identifies Correct Twin by Gait
- **Setup:** In a Milwaukee county courthouse, twin brother Darius Cole appears at the sentencing hearing for an assault his identical twin Marcus committed. Marcus coached Darius to take the plea — promising he'd "make it right" later.
- **Clue:** The victim, a restaurant owner named Helen Yoo, watches Darius walk from the holding area to the defense table and immediately knows something is wrong — the gait is different, the left hand is dominant instead of right, and the man sitting down isn't the one who attacked her.
- **Turn:** Helen stands and tells the judge: "That is not the man who hurt me. The man who hurt me drags his right foot and uses his right hand. This man is left-handed and walks evenly. He's the twin." The courtroom erupts.
- **Payoff (story):** The community rallies — Helen's testimony, a gait-analysis expert, and security footage comparison lead to Marcus's arrest. But the real payoff is Darius: freed from his brother's manipulation, supported by Helen and her neighbors who recognize that Darius was another of Marcus's victims. Found family emerges from the wreckage.
- **Lock:** Darius Cole 28 (wrong twin, orange jumpsuit, clean-shaven, left-handed, even gait, terrified eyes looking at the floor); Marcus Cole 28 (correct twin, not present in room — referenced only); Helen Yoo 52 (victim, dark blazer over a cream blouse, standing from the gallery bench, one hand raised, voice steady and clear); Judge Patricia Okafor 60 (black robe, reading glasses lowered, leaning forward from the bench). ### C32009 — F26-01 | Officer Separates Couple for Stokes Interview → Wife Answers Pet Name, Husband Fails Basic Facts
- **Setup:** At a USCIS field office in Denver, immigration officer Janet Ruiz separates married couple David Keane (US citizen) and Linh Pham (Vietnamese national, conditional green card) for a Stokes interview — the fraud-detection tool where couples must answer identical questions in isolation.
- **Clue:** Linh answers every question perfectly — pet names, first-date details, the color of their bedsheets, his mother's maiden name. David blanks on their anniversary, her birthday, and the name of her sister who lives with them.
- **Turn:** Janet calls them back together and places the answer sheets side by side. The discrepancy is undeniable. David looks at the floor. Linh looks at David and says, in English steadier than he expected: "I loved you. That part was never a lie."
- **Payoff (story):** The interview reveals what Linh already knew: David married her for the referral fee from an immigration agency, not for love. Linh genuinely fell in love during the marriage. The truth lands at the threshold — she passes the interview because her answers prove the marriage was real *to her*, but the relationship is over. Bittersweet: she keeps her status, but loses the man she thought she had.
- **Lock:** Linh Pham 30 (wife, modest navy dress, small gold earrings, hands folded on lap, composed with underlying heartbreak); David Keane 35 (husband, button-down shirt poorly ironed, avoiding eye contact, fidgeting with his wedding ring); Officer Janet Ruiz 45 (USCIS uniform blouse, badge, manila folder open, reading glasses, neutral professional expression shifting to empathy); Setting: USCIS field office interview room, Denver, CO, Thursday 2pm, government beige walls, two plastic chairs facing a metal desk, American flag pin on officer's collar, fluorescent light, one window with blinds half-closed. Object clue: two answer sheets side by side on the desk (text facing officer, not camera).

### C32010 — F29-11 | PTA Incumbent Runs Unopposed → Write-In Candidate Wins Through Overnight Coalition
- **Setup:** At Lakeview Elementary's annual PTA election, president Brenda Kirsch assumes her re-election is a formality — no one filed to run against her. She's controlled the budget, the fundraisers, and the volunteer lists for four years.
- **Clue:** Quiet parent Naomi Okafor has spent three weeks texting, calling, and meeting with every family in the school — especially the ones Brenda ignored: the night-shift parents, the ESL families, the single dads who were never invited to volunteer.
- **Turn:** At the cafeteria vote, Brenda gets 34 votes. The write-in line reads "Naomi Okafor" — 107 times. A coalition of parents who were never asked showed up because someone finally asked them.
- **Payoff (story):** Naomi's win isn't revenge — it's a circle closing. Twenty years ago, a PTA president at her own elementary school included Naomi's immigrant mother in every event, translated every flyer, saved a seat at every meeting. That woman's kindness shaped who Naomi became. Now Naomi does the same — generational kindness repaid through service to the next generation of overlooked families.
- **Lock:** Naomi Okafor 39 (write-in candidate, cardigan over a graphic tee, natural hair in a puff, warm confident smile, standing among a group of parents); Brenda Kirsch 45 (incumbent president, blowout hair, blazer with a PTA pin, holding a clipboard, stunned expression); Principal Diane Moss 55 (navy dress, reading election results from a folded paper at the podium, hint of a smile). Setting: elementary school cafeteria, Lakeview Elementary, Minneapolis, MN, Tuesday 7pm, folding tables pushed aside, plastic chairs in rows, hand-painted spirit banners on the walls, fluorescent light mixed with evening dark through high windows. Object clue: ballot box on the front table with folded papers overflowing.

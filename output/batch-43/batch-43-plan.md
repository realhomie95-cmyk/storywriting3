### C43001 | Fired Nephew → Trust Protector Holds the Freeze

**Seed:** F01-08 — A nephew is told he inherits the company → the trust protector arrives — the janitor he fired — with power to freeze every share

**Engine:** E00 — Humiliation → Reveal

**Arc:**
1. **Setup:** At the reading of patriarch Harold Mercer's will in a Charleston law office, nephew Trent Mercer is announced as sole inheritor of Mercer Maritime, a $90M shipping company. The room applauds.
2. **Clue:** The attorney pauses — there's a trust protector clause. The protector must co-sign any transfer. Trent laughs: "Who is it, some accountant?" The door opens.
3. **Turn:** Lionel Graves walks in — the building janitor Trent fired two months ago for "being too slow." Lionel is the trust protector Harold appointed fifteen years ago. He holds veto power over every share transfer, every board vote, every dividend.
4. **Payoff:** Lionel freezes all transfers pending a 90-day culture audit. Trent's first act as CEO would have been mass layoffs — Lionel blocks them. Harold knew his nephew, and he planned accordingly.

**Character Lock:**
- TRENT MERCER — 34, white, slicked chestnut hair, navy blazer with gold buttons, signet ring, smug posture; entitled, fired Lionel without cause
- LIONEL GRAVES — 63, Black, close-cropped silver hair, reading glasses on forehead, gray work shirt tucked into khakis; quiet, meticulous, former CPA who chose manual work after burnout; Harold's trusted friend for 30 years
- ATTORNEY MARGARET EAMES — 56, white, silver bob, black suit, bifocals on chain; neutral, reads the will
- Setting: Eames & Associates law office, 3rd floor, historic building on Broad Street, Charleston, SC; Tuesday 2 PM, afternoon sun through plantation shutters
- Object clue: trust protector clause document on the table; Lionel's building ID badge still clipped to his shirt

**Peak-tension (photo):** Trent standing at the conference table grinning with one hand on the will folder, attorney mid-sentence, Lionel entering through the glass door in his gray work shirt — Beat 2–3, twist NOT fully shown.

### C43002 | The Eulogy Ends → The Subject Clears His Throat

**Seed:** F04-03 — A brother finishes a glowing eulogy → the subject clears his throat in the back pew

**Engine:** E03 — Debt of Gratitude

**Arc:**
1. **Setup:** Vernon Oates, 68, is being eulogized at Greater Hope Baptist Church in Memphis by his younger brother Curtis. The church is packed — Vernon was a beloved mechanic who fixed cars for free in the neighborhood for 40 years.
2. **Clue:** Curtis mentions Vernon's "final gift" — a trust that pays for oil changes and brake jobs at the shop forever. A woman in the third pew clutches a thank-you card Vernon gave her daughter last month — signed and dated three days after his supposed death.
3. **Turn:** Curtis finishes. The organist begins. A voice from the back pew: "That was real nice, Curtis. But you got the part about my golf game wrong." Vernon stands, alive, thin but smiling. The church erupts.
4. **Payoff:** Vernon faked nothing — the hospital made an administrative error, declared him dead while he was in a coma from a stroke. He woke up four days later in a different ward. He came straight to his own funeral because "I wanted to hear what y'all really thought of me." The woman with the card — Denise Warren — is the one who called the hospital to correct the error. Vernon tells Curtis: "You don't remember her, but she's the girl whose transmission I rebuilt in '94 for free. She became a nurse. She found me."

**Character Lock:**
- VERNON OATES — 68, Black, wiry build, salt-and-pepper mustache, wearing a rumpled brown suit that's a size too big (hospital weight loss), walking cane; mechanic, generous, dry humor
- CURTIS OATES — 61, Black, stocky, bald, black suit with purple pocket square, emotional; Vernon's younger brother, gave the eulogy
- DENISE WARREN — 42, Black, nurse's scrubs under a gray cardigan, third pew, holding a thank-you card; the one who found Vernon alive
- Setting: Greater Hope Baptist Church, Orange Mound neighborhood, Memphis, TN; Saturday 11 AM, stained glass light, packed pews
- Object clue: thank-you card in Denise's hand dated after Vernon's "death"

**Peak-tension (photo):** Curtis at the podium mid-eulogy, tears on his face, congregation dabbing eyes — in the far back pew, a figure in a rumpled brown suit sits partially visible in shadow — Beat 2, twist NOT clearly shown.

### C43003 | Accent-Mocked Aide → State Inspector Documenting Neglect

**Seed:** F07-15 — Family complains about a new aide's accent → the aide is a state inspector documenting neglect

**Engine:** E09 — Slow-Burn Devotion

**Arc:**
1. **Setup:** Mirela Vasquez, 48, has been working as a nursing aide at Sunrise Manor in Albuquerque for six weeks. She speaks with a heavy accent. The adult children of resident Dorothy Sims — particularly son Bradley — have filed three complaints about her "inability to communicate."
2. **Clue:** Mirela carries a small leather notebook she writes in after every shift. She photographs meal trays, medication schedules, and staffing boards when no one is looking. She always stays fifteen minutes past her shift to sit with Dorothy.
3. **Turn:** After six weeks, Mirela doesn't show up for her shift. Instead, she arrives at the front desk in a pressed blazer with a state Department of Health lanyard. She is Inspector Mirela Vasquez-Torres, embedded for a compliance investigation triggered by an anonymous tip about understaffing and missed medications.
4. **Payoff:** The facility receives twelve citations. Bradley's complaints about Mirela's accent are entered as evidence of family disengagement — he never noticed his mother's bedsore, missed meds, or weight loss. Mirela personally ensures Dorothy is transferred to a better facility. The devotion was real — she stayed late because Dorothy reminded her of her own mother.

**Character Lock:**
- MIRELA VASQUEZ-TORRES — 48, Latina, dark hair in a tight bun, wire-rimmed glasses, speaks with a noticeable accent; state health inspector embedded as aide, carries a small leather notebook
- BRADLEY SIMS — 44, white, polo shirt, khakis, Bluetooth earpiece always in; visits monthly, complains loudly, never checks on mother's actual care
- DOROTHY SIMS — 81, white, thin silver hair, wheelchair, fading but alert; the resident Mirela quietly protects

- Setting: Sunrise Manor Assisted Living, Coors Blvd, Albuquerque, NM; weekday afternoon, fluorescent hallway light
- Object clue: small leather notebook Mirela writes in; state Department of Health lanyard hidden in her locker

### C43008 | Neighbors Mock Widow's Dusty Field → Geologist Confirms Formation Under Her Fence

**Seed:** F20-01 — Neighbors mock a widow's dusty field → geologist confirms a new formation under her fence line

**Engine:** E03 — Debt of Gratitude

**Arc:**
1. **Setup:** Loretta Hays, 72, owns 160 acres of scrubland outside Midland, Texas that hasn't produced since her husband Eugene died in 2011. Neighbors and the local oil co-op have offered pennies on the dollar for years. Her ranch hand's grandson, Marcus Hays (no relation), studied geology on a scholarship Eugene funded in 1998.
2. **Clue:** Marcus, now Dr. Marcus Hays, 34, drives out one Saturday with a seismic survey he ran on his own time. He unfurls the map on Loretta's kitchen table — there's a new formation, deeper than the old wells ever reached, directly under her fence line.
3. **Turn:** Loretta calls the co-op's bluff. She hires Marcus as her geologist. The test well hits at 8,400 feet. The co-op president — who'd been pressuring her to sell — watches from across the fence.
4. **Payoff:** Loretta's acreage is worth thirty times what they offered. She names the well "Eugene #1." Marcus tells her: "Your husband paid for my degree. I've been waiting ten years to pay it back." The scholarship fund Eugene started now covers twelve students annually — funded by royalties.

**Character Lock:**
- LORETTA HAYS — 72, white, sun-weathered face, silver braid under a straw hat, worn denim shirt, ranch boots; widow, stubborn, land-rich but cash-poor since Eugene died
- DR. MARCUS HAYS — 34, Black, close-cropped hair, wire glasses, plaid shirt with rolled sleeves, seismic map in hand; petroleum geologist, Eugene's scholarship recipient, no family relation to Loretta

## M031 | The Rivals Behind the Counter → A Forgery Only Two Eyes Could Catch

**Source:** Pawn Shops & Antiques (concepts3 #42-9) × E28 Unlikely Alliance (concepts4 #E28-9)  
**Engine:** E28 — Unlikely Alliance | **Tone:** mixed | **Photo:** twist-hidden  
**Opening style:** The return

### 4-Beat Arc

1. **Setup:** Virgil Odom, 72, a retired appraiser, walks into Gold Line Pawn in Birmingham AL to authenticate a Civil War-era pocket watch for the owner. He's met at the counter by Kenji Sato, 34, the shop's new head appraiser — who replaced Virgil two years ago in a dispute that ended their professional relationship.
2. **Clue/Build:** A high-dollar estate collection arrives for appraisal. Kenji spots something wrong with a porcelain piece but can't place it. Virgil notices the same flaw from across the counter — a glaze inconsistency only someone who handled the original molds would recognize. They trade reluctant glances.
3. **Turn:** The estate executor pressures a fast buy. Virgil and Kenji realize the entire lot is seeded with forgeries — and only by combining Virgil's historical knowledge and Kenji's materials science can they prove it before the shop loses six figures.
4. **Payoff:** They work the counter together through closing, assembling the proof. The fraud is stopped. Neither apologizes for the old rift — but Virgil leaves his loupe on the counter with a nod, and Kenji doesn't return it. The alliance is quiet, real, and unfinished.

### Character Lock

- **Virgil Odom** — 72, African-American, thin white mustache, reading glasses on a cord, brown tweed blazer over a plaid shirt, calloused hands, a brass loupe in his breast pocket
- **Kenji Sato** — 34, Japanese-American, clean-shaven, black hair swept back, fitted charcoal vest over rolled sleeves, a jeweler's scale always within reach
- **Setting** — Gold Line Pawn & Antiques, downtown Birmingham AL; narrow shop with glass display cases, brass instruments on walls, single overhead lamp casting warm pools, Wednesday late afternoon
- **Object clue** — Virgil's brass loupe (engraved with his initials — the one he left behind when he was pushed out)

### Peak-tension moment (photo)

Virgil and Kenji on opposite sides of the glass counter, both leaning in toward a porcelain piece under a desk lamp — not looking at each other, but their postures mirror. Virgil's hand hovers near the piece with his loupe; Kenji's jeweler's scale sits between them. The tension of old rivalry is visible in the stiff distance they keep. The collaboration/fraud reveal is NOT shown.

## M032 | The Janitor Who Watched Every Launch → He Left the Pad to Save His Son

**Source:** Space & Aerospace (concepts3 #37-12) × E27 The Protector Who Stayed Away (concepts4 #E27-4)  
**Engine:** E27 — The Protector Who Stayed Away | **Tone:** bittersweet | **Photo:** twist-hidden  
**Opening style:** Quiet devotion

### 4-Beat Arc

1. **Setup:** Tomás Reyes, 64, has worked as a pad janitor at Cape Canaveral for twenty-three years. His son, Mateo, 31, is a flight systems engineer at the same facility. They nod in the hallway but haven't spoken privately in eight years — since Tomás "abandoned" the family and moved to Florida without explanation.
2. **Clue/Build:** Mateo discovers his father's name on the employee roster dating back to just one month after he disappeared. A coworker mentions Tomás has never missed a launch Mateo worked — always watching from the cleaning crew's balcony. Mateo finds a locker with clippings of every mission his son touched.
3. **Turn:** Mateo confronts his father. Tomás reveals he left home in New Mexico because a former cartel connection from his youth resurfaced, threatening the family. He moved 1,800 miles away to draw the danger with him — and took the only job at the facility that would let him see his son every day without being close enough to bring the threat back.
4. **Payoff:** Mateo understands: his father didn't leave the family — he stood guard from the only distance that was safe. They don't hug. Tomás just says, "I saw every launch, mijo." Mateo nods. "I know." The distance closes — not all at once, but enough.

### Character Lock

- **Tomás Reyes** — 64, Latino, weathered brown skin, close-cropped gray hair, janitorial jumpsuit with a Cape Canaveral patch, a folded newspaper clipping in his chest pocket
- **Mateo Reyes** — 31, Latino, clean-cut, flight-systems polo and badge lanyard, resembles his father in the jaw and brow
- **Setting** — Kennedy Space Center service corridor, Cape Canaveral FL; fluorescent-lit hallway between the cleanroom and the viewing balcony, early morning before a launch window, pre-dawn blue through a far window
- **Object clue** — the newspaper clipping in Tomás's pocket (a photo of Mateo's first successful mission, cut from the Brevard County paper)

### Peak-tension moment (photo)

Tomás alone on the cleaning-crew balcony at pre-dawn, jumpsuit zipped, hands on the rail, gazing out toward the distant launch pad lit with floodlights. His chest pocket holds the folded clipping. The corridor behind him is empty. He is clearly watching something with quiet intensity — not working, just standing. The reveal of why he left is NOT shown.

## M033 | The Homemade Tools They Mocked → His Hands Save the Movement

**Source:** Jewelers & Watchmakers (concepts3 #43-9) × E22 Underdog Wins on Merit (concepts4 #E22-20)  
**Engine:** E22 — Underdog Wins on Merit | **Tone:** feel-good | **Photo:** action-in-photo  
**Opening style:** Crisis clock

### 4-Beat Arc

1. **Setup:** The American Horological Guild hosts its annual restoration challenge in Chicago — twenty watchmakers, one hour, one identical broken vintage movement each. Sponsors provide Swiss-made tool kits worth thousands. Pham Duc Loi, 58, a Vietnamese immigrant who repairs watches from a basement shop in Little Saigon, arrives with a rolled canvas of homemade tools — bamboo-handled picks, hand-ground screwdrivers, a loupe held together with electrical tape. The other competitors glance and smirk.
2. **Clue/Build:** Thirty minutes in, most competitors struggle with a seized mainspring barrel — the standard tools can't reach the recessed slot. Loi's hand-ground flat screwdriver, shaped over decades for exactly this geometry, fits perfectly. His hands move with a rhythm the crowd begins to notice.
3. **Turn:** With four minutes left, Loi sets the movement running. His is the first tick in the room. The judge's eyebrows lift. The sponsored pros are still working when the buzzer sounds.
4. **Payoff:** Blind grading. Loi's movement runs within COSC tolerance — the only one. He wins outright on craftsmanship, accuracy, and completion time. No secret past. No hidden credentials. Just forty years of work at a basement bench with tools he made himself.

### Character Lock

- **Pham Duc Loi** — 58, Vietnamese-American, wiry, reading glasses pushed up on forehead, gray-streaked black hair, short-sleeved white button-down tucked into brown slacks, a rolled canvas tool kit on his bench
- **Setting** — Hyatt Regency ballroom, Chicago IL; bright competition lighting, twenty identical workbenches in rows, a large digital clock on the back wall, Saturday midday
- **Object clue** — his hand-ground flat screwdriver (bamboo handle, blade shaped to fit a specific vintage barrel slot — forty years of use visible in the worn grip)

### Peak-tension moment (photo) — ACTION-IN-PHOTO

Loi at his bench mid-repair: loupe taped to his glasses frame, left hand steadying the movement with brass tweezers, right hand inserting the bamboo-handled screwdriver into the barrel slot. His rolled canvas kit is open beside him showing homemade tools. The competitors on either side work with gleaming sponsored kits. The digital clock shows time remaining. The winner announcement is NOT shown.

## M034 | The Driver Who Didn't Leave → Twelve Hours for a Stranger's Last Goodbye

**Source:** Rideshare & Taxi (concepts3 #15-4) × E18 The Stranger Who Stayed (concepts4 #E18-9)  
**Engine:** E18 — The Stranger Who Stayed | **Tone:** feel-good | **Photo:** twist-hidden  
**Opening style:** Tender moment

### 4-Beat Arc

1. **Setup:** Miriam Okafor, 47, a rideshare driver in Atlanta, picks up a quiet elderly man, Arthur Bowen, 81, for a hospital trip at 5:45 AM. His rating is five stars but he has no emergency contact listed. He asks to stop at a florist first. She notices his hands are shaking.
2. **Clue/Build:** At the hospital, Arthur asks Miriam to wait "just twenty minutes." She watches him enter the hospice wing carrying yellow tulips. Twenty minutes becomes an hour. She could leave. The app pings new rides. She declines them all. Through the glass, she sees Arthur sitting alone at a bedside, holding a woman's hand.
3. **Turn:** Three hours pass. Arthur emerges, eyes red, and says his wife just died. He has no one to call. The hospital asks for a family contact. He has none. Miriam turns off her app, helps him with paperwork, drives him to the funeral home, then to his empty house, then sits with him on his porch until his neighbor comes home at dusk.
4. **Payoff:** Twelve hours. Zero fares. Miriam stays until Arthur is not alone. She never tells him her reasons — she just stays, the way someone should. Arthur, walking her to the car at last, says only: "You didn't have to." Miriam says: "I know." That's enough.

### Character Lock

- **Miriam Okafor** — 47, Nigerian-American, warm brown skin, black braids pulled into a low bun, a beaded bracelet on her left wrist, gray zip-up hoodie over scrubs (she works a night shift at a care home, drives mornings)
- **Arthur Bowen** — 81, white, thin white hair combed neatly, tan cardigan over a pressed shirt, a wedding ring he keeps touching, holding yellow tulips wrapped in brown paper
- **Setting** — Miriam's rideshare sedan (silver Honda Accord, rosary on the mirror, a folded blanket on the back seat) parked under the porte-cochère of Emory Decatur Hospital, Atlanta GA; early morning, overcast, soft gray light
- **Object clue** — the yellow tulips (Arthur's wife's favorite; he brings them every visit — this is the last bouquet)

### Peak-tension moment (photo)

Miriam's sedan parked under the hospital porte-cochère, engine off, driver's door open. Miriam sits sideways in the driver's seat, feet on the curb, looking at her phone (declining a ride). Through the glass lobby doors behind the car, Arthur is barely visible — a silhouette sitting in the hospice hallway, tulips on the chair beside him. The gray morning light is flat and soft. The death/staying decision is NOT shown.

## M035 | The Barista They Laughed At → Her Latte Art Wins the National

**Source:** Coffee Shops & Cafés (concepts3 #45-12) × E22 Underdog Wins on Merit (concepts4 #E22-6)  
**Engine:** E22 — Underdog Wins on Merit | **Tone:** feel-good | **Photo:** action-in-photo  
**Opening style:** Crisis clock

### 4-Beat Arc

1. **Setup:** The US Barista Championship regional qualifier is held in Portland OR. Sixteen competitors from roasteries with R&D teams, custom machines, and branded uniforms. Rosa Delgado, 24, enters representing her family's bodega in East Portland — she learned latte art from YouTube videos and practices with a $200 secondhand machine. The judges note her entry fee was paid in crumpled bills.
2. **Clue/Build:** First round, Rosa's milk texturing is unconventional — she stretches longer than textbook, pours closer to the surface. Judges mark it unusual. A sponsored competitor beside her whispers "cute." But her rosetta is symmetrical to the millimeter, and her free-pour tulip has depth the others can't match.
3. **Turn:** Finals. Three pours, blind-scored. Rosa's last pour — a free-pour phoenix — draws a gasp from the crowd before the judges even score it. Her hand is steady, her breathing visible, and the design emerges in a single unbroken motion.
4. **Payoff:** Scores posted. Rosa's combined technical and artistic marks are the highest in the qualifier's history. She wins. No sponsor. No custom machine. No secret mentor. Just ten thousand hours of practice on a secondhand Breville in a bodega back room.

### Character Lock

- **Rosa Delgado** — 24, Latina, dark hair in a tight ponytail under a black baseball cap, no makeup, her family bodega's name hand-stitched on a plain black apron, small silver crucifix at her throat
- **Setting** — Convention center stage, Portland OR; bright competition lighting, a long bar with three identical espresso machines, a crowd behind a velvet rope, large digital timer on the wall, Sunday afternoon
- **Object clue** — her black baseball cap (her late father's, she wears it at every pour — the brim is frayed and faded)

### Peak-tension moment (photo) — ACTION-IN-PHOTO

Rosa mid-pour at the competition bar: pitcher tilted at a precise angle, milk streaming into the cup in a thin controlled line, her eyes locked on the surface of the drink. Her plain black apron contrasts with the branded uniforms of competitors at adjacent stations. The digital timer glows behind her. Her left hand steadies the cup with visible calm. The score reveal/celebration is NOT shown.

## M036 | The Porter They Dismissed → He Owns the House That Sold Him Short

**Source:** Auction Houses (concepts3 #41-10) × E30 Full-Circle Role Reversal (concepts4 #E30-8)  
**Engine:** E30 — Full-Circle Role Reversal | **Tone:** mixed | **Photo:** twist-hidden  
**Opening style:** The return

### 4-Beat Arc

1. **Setup:** Whitmore & Associates, a prestigious auction house in Charleston SC, is hosting a charity gala lot — a rare first-edition set donated anonymously. The floor buzzes. In the back row sits Cyrus Webb, 56, in a quiet navy suit. Fifteen years ago, he was the house's porter — fired after a client accused him of damaging a piece he'd actually saved from a fall. No one investigated.
2. **Clue/Build:** The lot goes to bidding. Prices climb. Cyrus raises his paddle calmly, outbidding every collector in the room. Staff don't recognize him — he's older, composed, successful. But the head auctioneer, Gerald Whitmore, 70, notices the paddle number belongs to "Webb Holdings" and freezes mid-call.
3. **Turn:** Cyrus wins the lot. He walks to the podium to sign the purchase paperwork. Gerald recognizes him — the porter he never defended. The room watches Cyrus hold the pen, the power entirely his.
4. **Payoff:** Cyrus signs. He donates the set immediately back to the charity, doubling its value with the publicity. He shakes Gerald's hand — firm, not cruel — and says, "I always loved this house. I just needed to come back on different terms." Grace, not revenge. The circle closes.

### Character Lock

- **Cyrus Webb** — 56, African-American, silver temples, trimmed goatee, navy suit with a subtle pinstripe, gold cufflinks, a numbered auction paddle in his right hand
- **Gerald Whitmore** — 70, white, silver hair swept back, black tuxedo, reading glasses perched on his nose, standing at the auctioneer's podium
- **Setting** — Whitmore & Associates auction house, Charleston SC; grand ballroom with chandeliers, rows of gilded chairs, a mahogany podium, Friday evening gala, warm amber light
- **Object clue** — Cyrus's auction paddle (numbered 7 — the same number as his old porter badge)

### Peak-tension moment (photo)

Cyrus in the back row of the auction hall, paddle raised calmly in his right hand, face composed and unreadable. Around him, other bidders in evening wear turn to look. The auctioneer at the distant podium is mid-gesture, gavel poised. The warm chandelier light catches Cyrus's gold cufflinks. The recognition/donation moment is NOT shown.

## M037 | The Feuding Bakers at City Hall → A Flood Makes Them Partners

**Source:** City Hall & Local Politics (concepts3 #38-12) × E28 Unlikely Alliance (concepts4 #E28-29)  
**Engine:** E28 — Unlikely Alliance | **Tone:** mixed | **Photo:** twist-hidden  
**Opening style:** Room goes wrong

### 4-Beat Arc

1. **Setup:** A town council meeting in Cedar Falls IA erupts when two rival bakery owners — Jolene Pratt, 52, and Hanh Nguyen, 49 — both apply for the same city vendor contract for the centennial festival. They've undercut each other for a decade. The council can't decide. Tempers flare.
2. **Clue/Build:** That night, the Cedar River floods downtown. Both bakeries — on opposite ends of Main Street — take water. Jolene's ovens are submerged. Hanh's storefront is intact but her supply chain is cut. Neither can fulfill the contract alone. The festival is in nine days.
3. **Turn:** Hanh shows up at Jolene's gutted kitchen with her last fifty pounds of flour and a proposal: split the contract, share her working ovens, combine their recipes. Jolene's pride wars with her practicality. She looks at the mud on her floor and says, "Your sourdough or mine?"
4. **Payoff:** Nine days later they deliver together — Jolene's pies in Hanh's boxes, Hanh's bread on Jolene's trays. The festival crowd doesn't know the backstory. But the two women stand behind the same booth for the first time in a decade, and neither walks away. The rivalry doesn't end — it just finds a new shape.

### Character Lock

- **Jolene Pratt** — 52, white, sandy blonde hair in a messy bun, flour-dusted denim apron over jeans, work boots, arms crossed, a smear of mud on her forearm
- **Hanh Nguyen** — 49, Vietnamese-American, black hair under a bandana, a clean white chef's coat (her kitchen survived), carrying a paper sack of flour
- **Setting** — Cedar Falls IA city council chamber (fluorescent lights, folding chairs, a raised dais with microphones) transitioning to Jolene's flooded bakery kitchen (mud line on walls, overturned racks, one working outlet)
- **Object clue** — the paper sack of flour Hanh brings (her last fifty pounds, stamped with her supplier's logo — the peace offering)

### Peak-tension moment (photo)

The city council chamber mid-argument: Jolene standing at the public-comment microphone, one hand gesturing sharply; Hanh seated three rows back, arms crossed, jaw set, staring at Jolene's back. Council members at the dais look uncomfortable. Folding chairs between them are half-empty. The flood/alliance is NOT shown.

## M038 | The Dentist's Daughter Never Knew → He Built the Ramp She Couldn't See

**Source:** Dental Clinics (concepts3 #27-11) × E27 The Protector Who Stayed Away (concepts4 #E27-15)  
**Engine:** E27 — The Protector Who Stayed Away | **Tone:** bittersweet | **Photo:** twist-hidden  
**Opening style:** Quiet devotion

### 4-Beat Arc

1. **Setup:** Dr. Nina Vasquez, 33, runs a small dental clinic in Albuquerque NM. She's used a wheelchair since a car accident at fifteen. Her estranged father, Esteban, 62, hasn't been in her life since the divorce when she was eight. She tells people she built everything herself — and believes it.
2. **Clue/Build:** The building's landlord mentions offhand that "the man who paid for the accessibility renovations" called again to check on the ramp condition. Nina has always assumed the city grant covered it. She digs into records: no grant exists. The payments come from a trust in her father's name, established the year of her accident.
3. **Turn:** Nina finds Esteban's name on eighteen years of anonymous payments: the wheelchair ramp, the adjusted operatory chairs, the wide-door renovation, even the parking-lot restriping. He's been engineering her independence from a distance — building the infrastructure of her life without ever stepping into it.
4. **Payoff:** Nina drives to the return address on the trust paperwork — a small apartment across town. Esteban opens the door. He doesn't explain or apologize. He just looks at her and says, "You did everything yourself. I just made sure the doors were wide enough." She doesn't forgive him yet. But she doesn't drive away.

### Character Lock

- **Esteban Vasquez** — 62, Latino, graying black hair, a day's stubble, denim work jacket, paint-stained hands (he works construction), a photo of Nina's clinic tucked in his wallet
- **Dr. Nina Vasquez** — 33, Latina, dark hair in a professional low ponytail, white dental coat, seated in a lightweight manual wheelchair, a determined set to her jaw
- **Setting** — Nina's dental clinic, Albuquerque NM; a clean single-story building with a wide concrete wheelchair ramp, Southwest light through large windows, Wednesday morning; later, Esteban's sparse apartment doorway
- **Object clue** — the wheelchair ramp itself (poured concrete, well-maintained, no plaque or name — anonymous by design)

### Peak-tension moment (photo)

The exterior of Nina's clinic at morning golden hour: the wide concrete wheelchair ramp in the foreground, freshly swept. Through the glass door, Nina is visible inside at her desk, working, unaware. Across the parking lot, barely visible at the edge of frame, a man in a denim work jacket stands beside a pickup truck, looking at the building — not entering. The ramp connects them and separates them. The discovery of his payments is NOT shown.

## M039 | The Painting Everyone Wanted → She Let It Go to Save a Stranger's Name

**Source:** Funerals & Estate (concepts3 #08-4) × E14 The Sacrificial Choice (concepts4 #E14-15)  
**Engine:** E14 — The Sacrificial Choice | **Tone:** mixed | **Photo:** twist-hidden  
**Opening style:** Tender moment

### 4-Beat Arc

1. **Setup:** At an estate sale following a reclusive collector's death in Savannah GA, the crown jewel is a small oil painting — unsigned, possibly a lost American Impressionist worth hundreds of thousands. Renée Acheson, 41, a gallery owner, has the winning bid locked in. This piece would save her struggling gallery.
2. **Clue/Build:** While examining the lot's provenance papers, Renée finds a faded letter tucked behind the frame: the painting was a gift to the deceased from a woman named Opal Jennings — a Black artist whose work was stolen and sold under white names in the 1940s. The painting is Opal's. The signature was scraped off.
3. **Turn:** Renée can buy it, resell it, save her gallery — no one would know. Or she can yield her bid, present the letter to the estate executor, and fight to have Opal's name restored. She chooses the name. She withdraws her paddle.
4. **Payoff:** The painting is pulled from sale. A museum investigation begins. Renée's gallery closes three months later — but Opal Jennings gets her first posthumous exhibition the following year, and the painting hangs with her name finally on the wall. Renée stands in that gallery at the opening, broke and at peace.

### Character Lock

- **Renée Acheson** — 41, white, auburn hair in a low chignon, black blazer over a cream blouse, reading glasses in hand, an auction catalog rolled in her other hand
- **Setting** — Telfair estate sale, a historic home in Savannah GA; high-ceilinged parlor with draped furniture, paintings leaning against walls, soft afternoon light through plantation shutters, Saturday
- **Object clue** — the faded letter behind the frame (handwritten in blue ink on onionskin paper, signed "Opal," dated 1947)

### Peak-tension moment (photo)

Renée alone in the parlor, holding the small oil painting up to the window light with both hands, examining the lower-right corner where a signature should be — the scraped surface barely visible. Her auction catalog is tucked under her arm. Behind her, other lots lean against the wall. Her expression is conflicted — brow furrowed, lips parted, caught between want and conscience. The letter and her decision are NOT shown.

## M040 | The Custodian Who Opened the Library → A Shared Cab Comes Back as a Scholarship

**Source:** Graduations & Award Ceremonies (concepts3 #23-9) × E01 Kindness Rewarded (concepts4 #E01-28)  
**Engine:** E01 — Kindness Rewarded | **Tone:** feel-good | **Photo:** twist-hidden  
**Opening style:** Moral choice

### 4-Beat Arc

1. **Setup:** Commencement day at Georgia State University, Atlanta. Aaliyah Monroe, 22, is about to cross the stage summa cum laude in English. What the audience doesn't know: she almost didn't graduate because she couldn't access the library after hours her freshman year — until the night custodian, Mr. James Osei, 63, started letting her in and leaving the lights on.
2. **Clue/Build:** Four years ago, Aaliyah shared a cab with a stranger during a rainstorm — a quiet older woman who forgot her wallet. Aaliyah paid the fare without thinking. The woman, Dr. Constance Reed, 71, a retired professor and anonymous donor, never forgot the girl in the rain. She quietly funded a "merit scholarship" that landed in Aaliyah's account the next semester — the money that kept her enrolled.
3. **Turn:** At the ceremony, Aaliyah is named the recipient of the Reed Endowment Prize — a new award she's never heard of. Dr. Reed stands in the audience. Aaliyah doesn't recognize her. But Dr. Reed holds up a worn taxi receipt, and the dean reads: "For the young woman who paid a stranger's fare and never asked why."
4. **Payoff:** Aaliyah's hand goes to her mouth. She looks at the woman — and remembers the rain, the cab, the forgotten wallet. A five-dollar kindness circled back as four years of tuition and a prize that will fund the next student. The custodian, Mr. Osei, watches from the back row in his work khakis. Two kindnesses, two strangers, one graduate.

### Character Lock

- **Aaliyah Monroe** — 22, African-American, natural hair in a crown of twists under her mortarboard, gold honor cord, a simple white dress under her gown, silver stud earrings
- **Dr. Constance Reed** — 71, African-American, silver-gray natural hair cropped short, elegant navy dress, pearl earrings, holding a worn taxi receipt in her hand
- **Mr. James Osei** — 63, Ghanaian-American, graying close-cropped hair, work khakis and a pressed short-sleeve button-down, sitting in the back row
- **Setting** — Georgia State University commencement, Atlanta GA; outdoor stadium, blue-and-white banners, rows of folding chairs on the field, bright Saturday morning, May heat
- **Object clue** — the worn taxi receipt (Dr. Reed has carried it for four years — date, fare amount, and Aaliyah's first name scribbled by the driver)

### Peak-tension moment (photo)

Aaliyah in her cap and gown walking across the outdoor stage toward the podium, gold honor cord catching the sunlight, one hand adjusting her mortarboard. The audience behind her is a blur of faces. In the far back row, Mr. Osei is visible in his work khakis, hands clasped on his knees, watching. Dr. Reed is somewhere in the middle rows — not yet identified. The prize announcement and recognition are NOT shown.

I couldn't breathe.

He knew.

He was testing us.

Victor never looked. Victor saw frames and dust and square footage being wasted. He saw a liquidation opportunity — forty-two canvases at ten dollars apiece. Maybe five hundred for the lot if the auction house was feeling generous.

The painting was authentic. The canvas weave matched his known supplier. The pigments were consistent with his 1835–1837 palette. The brushwork was unmistakably his.

Valuation: $14.2 million.

I called Victor that night.

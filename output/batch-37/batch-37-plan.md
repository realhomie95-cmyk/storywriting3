### C37001 | Worthless Paintings → Missing Master Worth Millions

**Seed:** F01-17 — Relatives fight over worthless paintings → the appraiser identifies one canvas as a missing master worth the entire estate

**Engine:** E00 — Humiliation → Reveal

**Arc:**
1. **Setup:** The Navarro family gathers at a Columbus, Ohio estate lawyer's office to divide their late patriarch's belongings. His art collection — forty-two canvases stored in a rented warehouse — is dismissed as "garage sale junk" by eldest son Victor, who wants to auction everything for scrap.
2. **Clue:** Youngest daughter Lucia, 34, an art history adjunct, notices brushwork on a small landscape that doesn't match amateur technique. She asks for a formal appraisal. Victor laughs and calls her "the professor with no money."
3. **Turn:** The certified appraiser arrives mid-meeting and identifies the landscape as a lost Thomas Cole painting from 1836 — authenticated, valued at $14.2 million. It alone exceeds the rest of the estate combined. The will grants the art collection solely to "whichever heir requests proper evaluation."
4. **Payoff:** Lucia inherits the entire collection. Victor's attempt to fast-sell is voided. The painting is loaned to the Columbus Museum of Art under a trust bearing their father's name — exactly what he wanted all along.

- VICTOR NAVARRO — 48, Latino, broad-shouldered, graying goatee, navy suit no tie, gold watch; eldest son, real estate investor, dismissive of art

- Setting: Kellner & Associates law office, 5th floor conference room, downtown Columbus, OH; Tuesday afternoon, gray winter light through venetian blinds

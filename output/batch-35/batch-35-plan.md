### C35001 | F01-08 | The Trust Protector — Nephew Inherits Company → Janitor Holds Veto

**Seed:** A nephew is told he inherits the company → the trust protector arrives — the janitor he fired — with power to freeze every share

**Engine:** E04 — The Test in Disguise

**Arc:**
1. **Setup:** Brent Calloway, 34, strides into the probate reading at Calloway & Sons, Charleston, SC, expecting sole inheritance of his late uncle's manufacturing firm. The family attorney congratulates him.
2. **Clue:** Attorney mentions the trust protector must co-sign the transfer. Brent asks who it is. The attorney pauses — the protector is identified only by employee number 0047 on a sealed 2009 addendum.
3. **Turn:** The conference room door opens. Luther Mays, 61, the custodian Brent fired two weeks ago for "restructuring," walks in wearing his navy work shirt and sets a single key on the table — the trust protector's authentication token.
4. **Payoff:** Luther freezes all shares until Brent rehires the twelve workers he let go. Brent's uncle made Luther protector decades ago because Luther once returned a briefcase of cash left on a bus. The shares remain frozen until Brent complies — or forfeits to a scholarship fund Luther administers.

**Character Lock:**
- BRENT CALLOWAY — 34, white, slicked brown hair, fitted charcoal suit, gold cufflinks, cocky stance; nephew and presumed sole heir
- LUTHER MAYS — 61, Black, close-cropped gray hair, navy custodian work shirt with name patch, reading glasses in breast pocket, calm steady posture; trust protector, former custodian
- VIVIAN CROSS — 58, white, silver bob, tailored burgundy blazer, wire-rim glasses; estate attorney, neutral
- Setting — Calloway & Sons conference room, King Street law office, Charleston, SC; Wednesday 10 AM, tall windows with white plantation shutters, polished mahogany table
- Object clue — small brass key (trust protector authentication token) that Luther places on the table

**Arc:**

2. **Clue:** Sister Elise Vane, 44, has said nothing. She sets a binder on the table — two years of home health aide logs, visit records, pharmacy runs, and overnight-stay timestamps, all in her name.

- RUSSELL VANE — 48, white, ruddy cheeks, leather jacket, silver watch; brother, loud, claims credit

**Seed:** Company threatens eminent domain → easement map crosses protected aquifer — widow owns water rights

**Engine:** E06 — Quiet Competence (Right All Along)

**Arc:**
1. **Setup:** Marisol Fuentes, 68, sits alone at her kitchen table on a 400-acre ranch outside Midland, TX, reading a letter from Permian Conduit LLC demanding easement access or threatening eminent domain proceedings.
2. **Clue:** A dusty rolled map tube sits on top of her late husband's filing cabinet. Inside is a 1974 aquifer protection survey with the Fuentes name circled as the registered water-rights holder for the entire Ogallala draw beneath the proposed pipeline route.
3. **Turn:** At the county hearing, Permian Conduit's attorney presents their route. Marisol's attorney unrolls the 1974 survey. State water code prohibits easement over a protected aquifer without the registered rights-holder's written consent — which Marisol has never given.
4. **Payoff:** The pipeline is rerouted at Permian's expense. Marisol's water rights, worth more than the land itself, remain intact. She rolls the map back into its tube and drives home to a ranch that will outlast the oil boom.

**Character Lock:**
- MARISOL FUENTES — 68, Mexican-American, silver-streaked black hair in a low twist, weathered face, pearl-snap Western shirt, worn ranch boots; widow, quiet, precise with paperwork
- DALE ECKHART — 45, white, crew cut, navy polo with Permian Conduit logo, cowboy boots too clean for ranch work; land agent, aggressive

### C35009 | F26-01 | The Stokes Interview — Officer Separates Couple → Wife Answers, Husband Fails

**Seed:** Officer separates couple for questions → wife answers pet name — husband fails basic facts

**Engine:** E04 — The Test in Disguise

**Arc:**
1. **Setup:** At the USCIS field office in Miami, FL, Immigration Officer Carmen Reyes separates Alejandra Moya, 31, and her American husband, Tyler Egan, 33, for their Stokes marriage-fraud interview.
2. **Clue:** Alejandra answers every question — the color of their bedsheets, Tyler's childhood dog's name, the pet name she calls him at night. Tyler stumbles on her birthday, names the wrong color for the couch, and cannot recall their wedding song.
3. **Turn:** Officer Reyes reconvenes them. She places Tyler's answers next to Alejandra's. The mismatch ratio triggers a fraud referral — not against Alejandra, but against Tyler. His prior petition history surfaces: two previous marriage-based petitions for other women, both withdrawn.
4. **Payoff:** Alejandra is offered a Violence Against Women Act (VAWA) self-petition path — the marriage was real on her side, and Tyler's pattern constitutes immigration fraud against her. Tyler faces federal charges. Alejandra keeps her green card process intact, free of him.

**Character Lock:**
- ALEJANDRA MOYA — 31, Colombian-American, dark waves past shoulders, cream blouse, small gold cross necklace, hands clasped tight; genuine spouse, nervous but truthful
- TYLER EGAN — 33, white, cropped brown hair, blue button-down rolled at sleeves, fidgeting with a pen; husband, serial petitioner, overconfident

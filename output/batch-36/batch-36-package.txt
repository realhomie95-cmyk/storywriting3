Devon hasn't spoken to me since.

"Your uncle would be proud. I am too."

I framed it. It sits on my desk beside a photo of Uncle Reg at the original warehouse in 1974 — young, lean, holding a clipboard and squinting into the sun.

He built something extraordinary. And he made sure the right person would protect it.

Not the one who wanted power.

The one who earned trust.

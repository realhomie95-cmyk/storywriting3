## 4-beat arcs + Character Lock

### C49001 — F01-07 | Siblings Jeer at Camcorder Tape → Mother Disinherits the Bully, Gifts Diner to the Waitress
- **Setup:** Three siblings gather in the law office for a will reading; they mock an old camcorder tape the lawyer sets up.
- **Clue:** The tape is labeled in their late mother's handwriting; one sibling, Kevin, rolls his eyes and reaches to eject it.
- **Turn:** The recording shows their mother, clear-eyed and composed, naming Kevin's decades of cruelty and disinheriting him.
- **Payoff:** The family diner and its trust go to Rosa, the waitress who cared for the mother in her final years, now seated quietly in the back row.
- **Lock:** Kevin Maddox 44 (eldest son, expensive watch, charcoal sport coat, smirk); Jenna Maddox 39 (middle daughter, neutral blazer, arms crossed); Tyler Maddox 35 (youngest, hoodie under jacket, uncomfortable); Rosa Calderon 52 (waitress, simple navy dress, hands folded in lap, back-row seat); Attorney Bev Howell 60 (reading glasses, manila folder). Setting: Wood-paneled law office, Charleston, SC, Wednesday 2pm. Object clue: old camcorder on the mahogany desk, red record light dark.

### C49002 — F04-03 | Brother Finishes Glowing Eulogy → The Subject Clears His Throat from the Back Pew
- **Setup:** Deacon Marcus Holt finishes a tearful eulogy for his younger brother Terrence, killed in a reported accident.
- **Clue:** The casket is closed; the funeral program lists no viewing. A stranger in the last pew keeps his collar up.
- **Turn:** As the organ plays out, a voice from the back says, "Marcus. I'm here." Terrence stands, alive, thin, different.
- **Payoff:** Terrence faked his death to escape a debt ring that Marcus unknowingly funded; the reunion is raw, unresolved, public.
- **Lock:** Marcus Holt 48 (deacon, black suit, silver tie pin, tears on face, at the pulpit); Terrence Holt 41 (gaunt, gray peacoat with collar turned up, week-old beard, back pew); Darlene Holt 74 (mother, front pew, floral hat, tissue); funeral guests in Sunday clothes. Setting: Mt. Olive Baptist Church, Memphis, TN, Saturday 11am, stained glass light. Object clue: closed casket with a single white lily on top.

### C49003 — F06-14 | Wealthy Members Reserve Front Rows → The Usher They Moved Wrote the Check That Rebuilt the Steeple
- **Setup:** At Cornerstone Community Church, wealthy donor families claim front pews and push long-time usher Clarence to the back.
- **Clue:** A brass plaque near the new steeple entrance reads "Anonymous Donor — 2021." Clarence polishes it every Sunday.
- **Turn:** When the building committee president thanks the anonymous donor publicly, the pastor reveals it was Clarence all along.
- **Payoff:** The congregation rises; Clarence is invited to sit wherever he wants; he chooses his usual spot — the back — and smiles.
- **Lock:** Clarence Mosby 71 (usher, pressed khaki pants, white short-sleeve shirt, name tag, calm demeanor); Evelyn Pratt 55 (building committee president, tailored green dress, pearl earrings, microphone); Pastor Daniels 62 (salt-and-pepper beard, clerical collar). Setting: Cornerstone Community Church, Boise, ID, Sunday 10am, morning light through tall windows. Object clue: brass "Anonymous Donor — 2021" plaque beside the entrance arch.

### C49004 — F09-10 | Tutor's Purse Holds a Key Labeled with His Pet Name → Wife Finds the Key and Ends It All at the Kitchen Table
- **Setup:** Megan Tate discovers a house key in the tutor's purse when looking for her son's worksheet — the key fob is labeled "Bear," her husband's private nickname.
- **Clue:** Late tutoring sessions, always after bedtime; Jordan always leaves through the back door; the key matches their front lock.
- **Turn:** Megan sets the key on the kitchen table and waits. When her husband Kyle walks in, she says one sentence: "Explain Bear."
- **Payoff:** Kyle cannot explain; Jordan is gone that night; the marriage unravels at the table in real time, and Megan holds every card.
- **Lock:** Megan Tate 36 (wife, dark ponytail, oversized cardigan, exhausted calm); Kyle Tate 38 (husband, loosened tie, briefcase in hand, frozen in doorway); Jordan Voss 28 (tutor, not present in photo — referenced). Setting: Suburban kitchen, Naperville, IL, Tuesday 9pm, overhead pendant light. Object clue: single brass key on a leather fob labeled "Bear" lying on the white quartz countertop.

### C49005 — F12-22 | Stepparent Given Last-Row Tickets → Donor Board Shows Stepparent Named the Scholarship

- **Payoff:** Hailey runs from the stage to the back row; Craig watches from the front, silent; the auditorium cheers.
- **Lock:** Dean Pryor 45 (stepfather, khaki pants, blue button-down, seated alone in back row, slight smile); Hailey Pryor 18 (graduate, maroon cap and gown, hands over mouth); Craig Sutter 46 (bio-father, front-row seat, polo shirt, arms crossed). ### C49006 — F14-08 | Hospital Follows Intended Parents' Birth Plan → Surrogate's Medical POA Enters and Overrides

- **Lock:** Amara Diallo 30 (surrogate/biological mother, hospital gown, braids pulled back, exhausted but resolute); Darren Kohl 42 (intended father, polo shirt, pacing); Lisa Kohl 40 (intended mother, cardigan, clutching binder); Attorney Rowan Beck 50 (gray suit, leather folder, standing in doorway). ### C49007 — F16-11 | Brother Auctions Farm Equipment Without Telling Sister → Buyer Is Sister's LLC, She Outbid Him
- **Setup:** Garrett Lund lists their late father's combine, tractor, and attachments at a county auction without consulting his sister Nora.
- **Clue:** An anonymous phone bidder has been topping every lot by exactly one increment. Garrett is grinning, counting cash in his head.
- **Turn:** The auctioneer reads the winning bidder's registered name: "Lund Heritage LLC." Nora steps out of the crowd holding the LLC papers.
- **Payoff:** Every piece returns to the family farm — under Nora's name. Garrett's greed funded nothing. The crowd applauds.
- **Lock:** Garrett Lund 47 (brother, dusty Carhartt jacket, trucker cap, hands on hips, stunned); Nora Lund-Reeves 43 (sister, flannel shirt, jeans, LLC folder in hand, composed); Auctioneer Hank Parsons 58 (cowboy hat, gavel, microphone). Setting: Open-air auction lot, Tulsa, OK, Saturday 10am, bright morning sun, equipment lined up. Object clue: gavel mid-swing in auctioneer's hand.

### C49008 — F20-06 | Pipeline Company Threatens Eminent Domain → Widow Owns the Water Rights That Void the Easement
- **Setup:** PetroLine Energy sends reps to a county hearing to push an easement across Jolene Carr's ranch for a crude pipeline.
- **Clue:** Jolene sits alone in the back with a single manila folder while the company's three lawyers dominate the podium.
- **Turn:** When invited to speak, Jolene opens the folder: she owns the water rights to the aquifer the pipeline would cross — rights senior to the state's easement power.
- **Payoff:** The easement is denied on environmental grounds Jolene's filing triggers; the company that once offered her pennies per acre now has no route. Jolene holds power with grace — she offers to negotiate, on her terms.
- **Lock:** Jolene Carr 58 (widow, weathered denim jacket, silver braid, turquoise ring, manila folder); Brad Cutler 45 (PetroLine lead counsel, navy suit, confident stance at podium); County Judge Elaine Foss 63 (bench, reading glasses, gavel). Setting: County hearing room, Midland, TX, Thursday 9am, fluorescent panel lights, wood-paneled walls. Object clue: manila folder with a blue water-rights tab on Jolene's lap.

### C49009 — F24-09 | Wrong Twin Appears for Sentencing → Victim Identifies Correct Twin by Gait
- **Setup:** Identical twins Marcus and Darnell Price — Marcus convicted of assault, Darnell serving time in his place to protect his brother.
- **Clue:** The victim, Sheila Vance, watches from the gallery as "Marcus" walks to the defense table — something in the walk is wrong.
- **Turn:** Sheila stands and tells the judge: "That's not him. The man who hit me drags his left foot." The judge orders both twins brought in.
- **Payoff:** Darnell's sacrifice is exposed; Marcus is arrested in the hallway. Bittersweet — Darnell loved his brother enough to take years; now both face consequences.
- **Lock:** Darnell Price 29 (twin in defendant's seat, orange jumpsuit, clean shave, steady gait); Marcus Price 29 (identical twin, hallway, slight left-foot drag, handcuffed mid-reveal); Sheila Vance 34 (victim, gallery seat, floral blouse, standing with one hand on rail); Judge Patricia Odom 56 (robe, leaning forward). Setting: Chatham County Courthouse, Savannah, GA, Monday 9am, high ceilings, dark wood. Object clue: defendant's ankle monitor visible under jumpsuit cuff.

### C49010 — F29-05 | PTA Queen Rigs Fundraiser Auction → Quiet Mom's Handmade Quilt Outsells Everything
- **Setup:** PTA president Gloria Fenn stacks the school auction with her friends' luxury items and buries quiet mom Sonia Park's handmade quilt in the last lot.
- **Clue:** The quilt is stitched with the school motto and every graduating class's handprints from the past decade.
- **Turn:** Bidding on the quilt explodes — parents recognize their children's tiny handprints. It outsells every luxury lot combined.
- **Payoff:** The community rallies around Sonia; Gloria's rigged pricing is exposed when the treasurer reads actual costs aloud; Sonia is voted onto the board.
- **Lock:** Gloria Fenn 44 (PTA president, silk blouse, clipboard, tight smile turning sour); Sonia Park 38 (quiet mom, denim jacket, hands clasped, tears forming); Treasurer Mike Olea 40 (polo shirt, reading glasses, printout in hand). Setting: Ridgeline Elementary gym, Boulder, CO, Friday 6pm, crepe-paper decorations, folding chairs. Object clue: handmade quilt draped over a display easel with colorful child handprints.

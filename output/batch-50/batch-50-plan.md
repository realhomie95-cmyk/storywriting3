## 4-beat arcs + Character Lock

### C50001 — F02-10 | CEO Dismisses Janitor's Claim → DNA Proves They Are Twins Separated at Birth
- **Setup:** A janitor named Marcus Cole approaches the CEO of a biotech company in the corporate lobby, claiming they are related. Security is called.
- **Clue:** Marcus holds a manila envelope with lab results from a consumer DNA test that flagged a close relative match.
- **Turn:** HR, forced by policy, runs a verification test — the markers confirm Marcus and CEO Adrian Voss are identical twins separated at a Soviet-bloc orphanage in 1986.
- **Payoff:** Adrian's constructed identity unravels; Marcus doesn't want money — he wants a brother; the board must decide how to handle a CEO whose entire origin story was a lie.
- **Lock:** Marcus Cole 38 (janitor, navy coveralls with name patch, close-shaved head, manila envelope in hand, calm dignity); Adrian Voss 38 (CEO, tailored charcoal suit, platinum watch, identical bone structure to Marcus, controlled rage masking fear); Security guard Darnell 45 (hand on Marcus's shoulder, earpiece). Setting: Glass-walled corporate lobby, Detroit, MI, Monday 9am, winter morning light. Object clue: manila envelope with lab logo visible.

### C50002 — F07-15 | Family Complains About New Aide's Accent → Aide Is a State Inspector Documenting Neglect
- **Setup:** The Rourke family visits their mother at Greenfield Nursing Home and loudly complains about the new aide's accent and "incompetence."
- **Clue:** The aide, Ines Salazar, carries a small notebook in her scrub pocket that she writes in during quiet moments.
- **Turn:** The facility director bursts into the day room — Ines is a state health inspector embedded undercover after three anonymous complaints. Her notebook is an official inspection log.
- **Payoff:** The facility faces citations for the neglect the family ignored; the Rourkes are shamed; Ines's documentation protects dozens of residents.
- **Lock:** Ines Salazar 42 (state inspector/undercover aide, teal scrubs, dark hair in a low bun, small spiral notebook in breast pocket, composed watchful eyes); Brenda Rourke 55 (daughter, camel coat, pointed finger, loud voice); Doris Rourke 82 (resident, wheelchair, thin hands gripping armrest); Director Hank Paulson 50 (sweating, loosened tie, entering from hallway). Setting: Nursing home day room, Tallahassee, FL, Wednesday 2pm, institutional fluorescent light mixed with window sun. Object clue: spiral notebook in Ines's scrub pocket.

### C50003 — F20-03 | Developer Offers Lowball for Ranch Land → The "Broke" Rancher Owns the Mineral Rights
- **Setup:** A Houston developer, Clayton Mercer, shows up at an aging rancher's fence line in Midland, TX, offering $80K for "useless scrubland" next to a new pipeline route.
- **Clue:** The rancher, Eula Vickers, stands with a folded survey map in her back pocket, listening without expression.
- **Turn:** Eula unfolds the map — it shows she owns not just the surface but the mineral rights beneath, worth millions. Clayton's pipeline cannot cross without her consent.
- **Payoff:** Clayton's lowball is exposed as a con; Eula negotiates from absolute strength; the "broke old woman" was the wealthiest person at the fence.
- **Lock:** Eula Vickers 71 (rancher, sun-faded denim shirt, straw hat, weathered hands, folded survey map in back pocket, squinting calm); Clayton Mercer 44 (developer, pressed khakis, aviator sunglasses pushed up, leaning on a white truck, contract folder in hand); Ranch hand Tomás 30 (background, leaning on fence post, arms crossed). Setting: Barbed-wire fence line, Midland, TX, Saturday 10am, flat scrubland, pump jacks visible in distance, blazing sun. Object clue: folded survey map in Eula's back pocket.

### C50004 — F10-04 | HR Insists Meeting Was Not Recorded → Employee Shares the Cloud Link
- **Setup:** After a wrongful termination, employee Jessie Okafor is told by HR director Pamela Cross that "no record exists" of the meeting where she was pressured to resign.
- **Clue:** Jessie's laptop shows a Zoom cloud recording auto-saved — HR forgot the platform's default setting.
- **Turn:** At the unemployment hearing, Jessie's attorney plays the recording: Pamela is heard saying "sign or lose your healthcare today."
- **Payoff:** Wrongful termination confirmed; Jessie receives back pay and damages; Pamela is placed on administrative leave.
- **Lock:** Jessie Okafor 34 (employee, natural hair in a puff, olive cardigan, laptop open showing a Zoom interface, steady hands); Pamela Cross 48 (HR director, blonde chignon, navy blazer, forced professional smile cracking); Attorney Reeves 55 (Jessie's lawyer, gray beard, reading glasses, one hand on a printout). Setting: Home office desk, Portland, OR, Thursday morning, rain-light through a window, laptop glow on face. Object clue: laptop screen showing cloud recording link (angled away from camera).

### C50005 — F21-07 | Creditor Slides an Envelope Across the Table → The Debtor Owns the Restaurant They're Sitting In
- **Setup:** Loan shark Vincent Pace slides a final-notice envelope to Sal Medina in a back booth, threatening to take everything.
- **Clue:** The waitstaff know Sal by name. The booth has a brass plate. The hostess nodded when Sal walked in.
- **Turn:** Sal opens the envelope, reads the amount, and calmly tells Vincent the debt was purchased yesterday — by the restaurant's owner. That owner is Sal.
- **Payoff:** Vincent's leverage evaporates; Sal bought his own debt at pennies on the dollar through an LLC; the predator is now the one who owes an explanation to his backers.
- **Lock:** Sal Medina 52 (debtor/secret owner, worn leather jacket over a button-down, silver at the temples, calm half-smile, envelope flat on the table under his palm); Vincent Pace 46 (creditor, black overcoat, slicked hair, gold pinky ring, leaning forward aggressively); Waitress Mona 35 (background, polishing a glass, watching). Setting: Dim back booth of a seafood restaurant, Atlantic City, NJ, Friday 9pm, red leather banquette, low pendant lamp, brass booth plate. Object clue: brass plate on booth wall + envelope under Sal's palm.

Object clue: laptop screen showing commit history (visible glow, not readable text).

### C50009 — F04-03 | Brother Finishes Glowing Eulogy → The Subject Clears His Throat in the Back Pew
- **Setup:** Warren Thibodaux delivers a tearful eulogy for his brother Caleb at a packed church funeral in Savannah.
- **Clue:** A man in the back pew, face obscured by a hat brim, shifts in his seat during the eulogy.
- **Turn:** As Warren says "I only wish I could hear his voice one more time," the man in the back stands and clears his throat. It is Caleb — alive.
- **Payoff:** Caleb faked his death to escape a business partner who was embezzling and threatening his family; the funeral was the trap to get the partner — seated in row four — to confess on a wire.
- **Lock:** Warren Thibodaux 45 (brother delivering eulogy, black suit, tears on cheeks, gripping podium); Caleb Thibodaux 42 (presumed dead, gray fedora pulled low, dark overcoat, standing in back pew); Business partner Gil Fontenot 50 (row four, sweating, loosened tie, hand in pocket). Setting: Historic church funeral hall, Savannah, GA, Saturday 11am, stained-glass light, closed casket at front, packed pews. Object clue: gray fedora pulled low obscuring face.

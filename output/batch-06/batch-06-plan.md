## C061 | Stepfather's Aisle Walk → Navy Chaplain Delivers Father's Tags

**Source:** Concept 01 #7 — The Military Chaplain's Promise  
**Opening style:** Silent witness  
**Setting:** San Diego Naval Memorial Chapel, San Diego, California — Saturday 3pm, ocean light through ship-motif stained glass

**4-beat arc:**
- **Setup:** Bride's wealthy stepfather walks her halfway down the aisle when an old Navy chaplain stands quietly at the back row, holding a navy velvet pouch.
- **Clue:** Chaplain steps forward without permission and lays a worn pair of dog tags on the altar table; bride sees the stamped name "PETERSEN — USS BURKE."
- **Turn:** Stepfather reaches to grab the tags; chaplain blocks him with one steady arm and recites a date — March 14, 2003 — that makes bride's mother in the front pew start crying.
- **Payoff:** Bride's biological father died on USS Burke in 2003; chaplain promised the dying sailor he would deliver the tags only when his daughter was about to walk into a future she had chosen freely; stepfather had hidden the chaplain's letters for 18 years to control the inheritance trust.

**Peak-tension moment (photo):** Old Navy chaplain in dress blacks placing dog tags on altar table; bride frozen halfway down aisle in ivory satin; stepfather gripping bride's elbow with mouth opening in protest; sunlight through ship-motif stained glass falling across teak pews.

**Character Lock:**
- Ava Petersen-Hale — 26, ash-blonde French braid, ivory satin gown, single pearl studs, hand resting on stepfather's arm
- Chaplain Daniel Reeve (USN ret.) — 73, dress blacks with white collar tab, weathered hands, navy velvet pouch in left hand, dog tags in right
- Gregory Hale (stepfather) — 58, charcoal suit, gold tie clip, possessive grip on Ava's elbow, mouth half-open in protest
- Setting — San Diego Naval Memorial Chapel, weathered teak pews, stained-glass windows with ship motifs, Saturday 3pm ocean light
- Object clue — worn dog tags stamped "PETERSEN — USS BURKE" in navy velvet pouch (pays off Act 3–4)

## C062 | Brooklyn Vendor Eviction → CEO's Grandmother's Sister Survived

**Source:** Concept 02 #14 — The Market Woman's Chant  
**Opening style:** Object clue  
**Setting:** Smith Street weekend market, Cobble Hill, Brooklyn, New York — Sunday 11am, low autumn sun between brick buildings

**4-beat arc:**
- **Setup:** Real-estate CEO tours his firm's "blighted block" with city planners and announces every market vendor must clear by Friday for luxury rezoning.
- **Clue:** An old produce vendor weighs apples and chants prices in three repeating notes; CEO's hand drifts to his temple — the song is the lullaby his grandmother sang the night she carried him from a 1972 Brooklyn fire.
- **Turn:** CEO sees a small charred tin ladle hooked to vendor's apron strap; he last saw that ladle in his grandmother's kitchen the night before the fire; vendor lifts her eyes calmly, no surprise.
- **Payoff:** Vendor is his grandmother's younger sister, presumed dead in the same fire; family told him she had perished; she had been chanting that lullaby in markets for forty years hoping a face would come back; CEO cancels rezoning, transfers block to community land trust in his grandmother's name.

**Peak-tension moment (photo):** Vendor in faded blue apron weighing apples on metal scale, lips parted mid-chant; CEO frozen mid-stride in charcoal overcoat with phone half-raised; city planner pointing at clipboard toward stalls; charred tin ladle catching low sun on apron strap.

**Character Lock:**
- Daniel Whittaker — 56, charcoal overcoat, leather gloves, phone in hand, frozen mid-stride
- Estelle "Granny Tess" Vargas — 81, gray bun under headscarf, faded blue apron, weathered hands at metal scale, charred tin ladle on apron strap
- Marisa Cruz (city planner) — 39, navy coat, clipboard, mid-gesture toward stalls
- Setting — Smith Street weekend market in Cobble Hill, Brooklyn, brownstone backdrop, Sunday autumn 11am, low sun between brick buildings
- Object clue — charred tin ladle hooked to apron strap (pays off Act 3–4)

## C063 | Drunk Gambler Throws Chips → Floor Attendant Owns the Block

**Source:** Concept 03 #15 — The Casino Floor Attendant  
**Opening style:** Dialogue slap  
**Setting:** Coastal Crown Casino high-limit table, Atlantic City, New Jersey — Friday 11:50pm, chandelier and slot-machine glow

**4-beat arc:**
- **Setup:** Drunk gambler at the high-limit baccarat table throws a stack of $5K chips at an elderly floor attendant for taking too long to swap markers, calls him "old man" and demands the pit boss replace him.
- **Clue:** Pit boss whispers urgently into earpiece, eyes on the security catwalk; attendant calmly bows to retrieve chips; security chief arrives at the table thirty seconds later, faster than protocol allows.
- **Turn:** Security chief addresses attendant by full name and asks, in front of the gambler, whether he wants the patron escorted out tonight or banned by morning.
- **Payoff:** Attendant is silent partner who owns Coastal Crown plus three other boardwalk casinos through a family trust; he works the floor monthly to test how staff and guests treat working people; gambler's marker is revoked, suite is emptied within the hour, name added to industry-wide no-comp list before sunrise.

**Peak-tension moment (photo):** Drunk gambler in open suit jacket mid-throw of chip stack across green felt; elderly attendant in vest bowed slightly to retrieve a chip from the carpet; pit boss with two fingers at earpiece staring past gambler; chandelier casting cold gold light across marble floor and slot glow.

**Character Lock:**

- Walter "Walt" Donato — 74, casino floor-attendant vest with name tag "Walt", silver hair, wire-frame glasses, calm posture, slight bow to retrieve chip
- Diane Russo (security chief) — 44, black blazer, earpiece coiled to collar, badge clipped at hip
- Setting — Coastal Crown Casino high-limit table, Atlantic City, NJ, Friday near midnight, chandelier light, marble floor reflections, slot-machine glow in background

## C064 | Banjo Busker in Courthouse → Judge Recognizes Carnival Bracelet

**Source:** Concept 04 #19 — The Carnival Bracelet  
**Opening style:** Visual reveal  
**Setting:** Davidson County Courthouse main hallway, Nashville, Tennessee — Tuesday 9am, gray morning light through tall arched windows

**4-beat arc:**
- **Setup:** A judge in robes hurries to chambers when a young street busker is caught by the bailiff just inside the courthouse door; the busker swears he was only sheltering from rain.
- **Clue:** Judge glances back, sees a faded orange-and-blue paper carnival bracelet on the busker's left wrist with "Marlow Family Carnival 1997" in cracked ink; judge stops breathing.
- **Turn:** Judge kneels in the hallway in his robes; busker steps back, recognizing nothing; judge whispers a name — "Eli" — that the busker has not heard since age four.
- **Payoff:** Busker is judge's son, taken at four during a custody escape and raised under a different surname; carnival bracelet was the last thing father tied on his wrist before disappearance; judge had bought a bracelet that day for himself and never taken it off either, hidden under his robe sleeve.

**Peak-tension moment (photo):** Bailiff with hand on busker's shoulder; busker holding battered banjo case, surprised eyes; judge in black robes frozen mid-step, eyes locked on faded paper bracelet at busker's wrist; marble columns and tall windows of courthouse hallway.

**Character Lock:**
- Judge Marcus Hollis — 61, black judicial robes, gray temples, reading glasses pushed up on forehead, hand pressed to chest
- Eli "Banjo Eli" Marlow — 30, denim jacket, road-worn boots, banjo case slung on shoulder, faded orange-and-blue paper carnival bracelet on left wrist
- Bailiff Gomez — 35, courthouse uniform, hand on Eli's shoulder, half-apologetic
- Setting — Davidson County Courthouse main hallway, Nashville, TN, Tuesday 9am, gray light through tall arched windows, marble columns, polished stone floor

## C066 | Veteran Smashes Museum Case → Recovers Tags Stripped in 1971

**Source:** Concept 06 #11 — The Glass Case Break  
**Opening style:** Deadline pressure  
**Setting:** U.S. Naval Academy Museum corridor, Annapolis, Maryland — Wednesday 11:55am, museum spotlights on glass display cases

**4-beat arc:**
- **Setup:** A frail elderly veteran pleads with the museum curator to retrieve dog tags from case 14B before the noon ribbon-cutting of the new "Forgotten Heroes" exhibit; curator refuses, citing protocol and committee schedule.
- **Clue:** Veteran sees the ribbon committee arriving with cameras at 11:55; he picks up a brass tour stanchion and breaks the glass with one controlled strike, kneels into the shards.
- **Turn:** Security swarms but stops short when they see the old man cradling the dog tags and weeping into the open case; curator finally reads the engraved name and goes pale.
- **Payoff:** Tags are his — surrendered after a 1971 court-martial for a rescue mission that violated orders and saved nineteen sailors; he was quietly cleared posthumously last year by a review board he never knew met; the museum was about to display the tags as a "captured artifact" with a wrongly attributed nameplate; curator pulls the exhibit, apologizes publicly at noon, ribbon committee reconvenes around the veteran.

**Peak-tension moment (photo):** Old veteran on knees in faded navy peacoat beside shattered display case, brass stanchion still in right hand, dog tags clutched against chest with left, head bowed; curator with lanyard frozen behind him hand half-raised; security guards rounding the corner; ribbon committee chairs visible in background corridor.

**Character Lock:**
- Joseph "Joe" Marek — 78, faded navy peacoat over wool sweater, gray beard, hands trembling but committed, brass tour stanchion in right hand, dog tags in left
- Dr. Laura Chen (curator) — 42, museum staff badge on lanyard, navy blazer, hand half-raised in shock
- Setting — U.S. Naval Academy Museum, Annapolis, MD, marble corridor with glass display cases under spotlight, Wednesday 11:55am, ribbon-cutting chairs and folded program in background
- Object clue — engraved dog tags "MAREK J. — USS HOLT" surrendered 1971 (pays off Act 3–4)

## C068 | Bikers Mock Veteran at Diner → Convoy Salutes Him Outside

**Source:** Concept 08 #1 — The Old Radio Call  
**Opening style:** Accuser POV  
**Setting:** Silver Bullet Diner, off I-80 outside Reno, Nevada — Sunday 1pm, harsh high-desert sun through dirty windows

**4-beat arc:**
- **Setup:** Three rough bikers humiliate an old veteran at the diner counter, snatch his cane, pour his coffee into his lap, and dare him to do something about it.
- **Clue:** Veteran calmly pulls a battered black handheld VHF radio from his coat pocket, presses one button, and says four words: "Reno checkpoint. Code Two." Bikers laugh.
- **Turn:** The parking-lot windows fill with the rumble of fifty bikes pulling in formation; lead rider walks into the diner, removes his helmet, and salutes the old man at the counter.
- **Payoff:** Veteran is retired Master Sergeant who founded the regional veterans-motorcycle escort association; bikers in the diner had been wearing knock-off patches of a chapter the veteran's network protected; lead rider revokes their colors on the spot, hands the cane back to the old man, and the diner waitress refuses their payment as they leave.

**Peak-tension moment (photo):** Three bikers in leather vests leaning over old veteran at chrome counter, lead biker holding stolen wooden cane crooked over shoulder; veteran with battered black handheld radio raised to his lips, face unreadable; spilled coffee pooling at counter edge; waitress in pink uniform frozen with order pad in mid-air.

**Character Lock:**
- Master Sgt. Hank "Top" Brogan (USA ret.) — 76, faded olive field jacket, ball cap with regimental crest, weathered hands, battered black handheld VHF radio raised to lips
- Three bikers — leather vests with patches, mid-30s to 40s, leader holding wooden cane crooked over shoulder
- Waitress June — 50s, pink diner uniform, frozen with order pad, mouth slightly open
- Setting — Silver Bullet Diner off I-80 outside Reno, NV, chrome counter and red-vinyl stools, Sunday 1pm, harsh high-desert sun through dirty windows, neon "OPEN" sign visible
- Object clue — battered black handheld VHF radio with regimental sticker on housing (pays off Act 3–4)

**Opening style:** Victim restraint  
**Setting:** Whitcombe beachfront mansion grand staircase, Naples, Florida — Saturday 6pm, gulf sunset through floor-to-ceiling windows

- **Setup:** A wealthy guest at an engagement party orders the elderly housekeeper to carry six matching designer bags up the marble staircase as the host watches without intervening; guest's fiancé winces but says nothing.
- **Clue:** Host's hand tightens white on the banister; guest's fiancée notices a framed family portrait at the staircase landing — the same elderly woman, twenty years younger, sitting in the master suite as the lady of the house.

## C070 | Busker Boy at Stage Door → Concertmaster Sees Brother in Photo

**Source:** Concept 10 #14 — The Photo in the Violin Case  
**Opening style:** Wrong apology  
**Setting:** Kansas City Symphony Hall backstage corridor, Kansas City, Missouri — Friday 7pm, dim warm hallway lamps before curtain

**4-beat arc:**
- **Setup:** Concertmaster snaps at a young busker boy who slipped past the stage door clutching a battered violin case to ask for an audition before the symphony's gala; concertmaster offers a forced apology only after staff hover, demands the boy be removed.
- **Clue:** Boy nervously kneels and opens his violin case to prove the instrument is real; a faded color photograph slides out across the carpet — a young man in tuxedo holding a baby outside this same symphony hall, dated 1998.
- **Turn:** Concertmaster picks up the photo with shaking hands; the man in the tuxedo is his missing younger brother who vanished in 1998 leaving an infant behind; he kneels beside the boy and asks his name.
- **Payoff:** Boy is the concertmaster's nephew, raised in foster care, taught violin by a retired symphony cellist who recognized the family resemblance and helped the boy track the photo back to the venue; concertmaster's real apology is for fifteen years of not searching hard enough; boy auditions in front of a private board the next morning and joins the youth program on full sponsorship.

**Peak-tension moment (photo):** Concertmaster in formal tuxedo mid-gesture pointing toward exit with one hand; boy kneeling on hallway carpet beside opened battered violin case; faded color photograph half-fallen onto carpet between them, baby's face partly visible; symphony posters and gold-framed conductor portraits on walls of dim corridor.

**Character Lock:**
- Concertmaster David Linwood — 49, formal tuxedo, silver-streaked hair, black bow tie, hand frozen mid-gesture toward exit
- Caleb "Cal" Linwood — 14, oversized winter coat, fingerless gloves, scuffed sneakers, kneeling beside opened battered violin case
- Setting — Kansas City Symphony Hall backstage corridor, Kansas City, MO, Friday 7pm, dim warm hallway lamps, framed symphony posters and conductor portraits on walls, carpet runner
- Object clue — faded color 1998 photograph of young man in tuxedo holding baby outside symphony hall (pays off Act 3–4)

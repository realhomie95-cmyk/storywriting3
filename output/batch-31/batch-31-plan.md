## 4-beat arcs + Character Lock summary

### C31001 — F02-10 | CEO Dismisses Janitor's Claim → DNA Proves They Are Twins Separated at Birth
- **Setup:** Janitor Marcus Webb confronts CEO Daniel Ashworth in the corporate lobby, claiming they share a father. Daniel laughs him off.
- **Clue:** Marcus holds an ancestry report and a hospital bracelet photo from 1986 — two male infants, same ward.
- **Turn:** Security-escorted out, Marcus's lawyer delivers a certified DNA comparison — full siblings, not half.
- **Payoff (story):** The orphanage record surfaces proving a deliberate separation; Daniel's "father" adopted only one twin; Marcus gains standing in the family trust.
- **Lock:** Marcus Webb 38 (janitor, olive work coveralls, hospital bracelet photocopy in hand, calm determination); Daniel Ashworth 38 (CEO, tailored charcoal suit, caught mid-laugh, coffee in hand); Security guard Ray 50 (background, hand on Marcus's shoulder). Setting: corporate lobby, glass atrium, Philadelphia, PA, Tuesday 8:30am, morning light through floor-to-ceiling windows. Object clue: hospital bracelet photocopy in Marcus's hand.

### C31002 — F06-14 | Wealthy Members Reserve Front Rows → The Usher They Moved Wrote the Check That Rebuilt the Steeple
- **Setup:** At Grace Baptist Church, wealthy donors insist on front-pew priority; volunteer usher Earl Combs is asked to move to the back.
- **Clue:** Earl's name appears nowhere on the donor wall, but the steeple fund records tell a different story.
- **Turn:** When the new pastor reads the building-fund history aloud during a dedication, Earl's anonymous gift — the single largest — is revealed.
- **Payoff (story):** The congregation realizes the quiet man in the back pew funded the very roof over their heads; Earl never wanted recognition, only a warm place for the community.
- **Lock:** Earl Combs 72 (usher, worn navy suit, polished shoes, arthritic hands folded, quiet dignity); Donor Patricia Vance 58 (silk blouse, pearl earrings, gesturing to front pew); Pastor Elijah Green 45 (new pastor, reading from a ledger at the pulpit). Setting: Grace Baptist Church sanctuary, Memphis, TN, Sunday 10am, stained-glass colored light, wooden pews, choir loft behind. Object clue: building-fund ledger open on pulpit (text not readable).

### C31003 — F09-24 | Seasonal Helper Wraps Gifts in the Den → Her Apron Pocket Holds a Matching Wedding Band
- **Setup:** Wife Carla Voss hires a "holiday helper" to wrap Christmas gifts while she's at work. Husband Dean insisted on the hire.
- **Clue:** The helper's apron pocket holds a wedding band identical to Dean's — same engraving, same jeweler.
- **Turn:** Carla discovers the ring when the helper leaves it on the counter; jeweler confirms two identical bands ordered on the same date.
- **Payoff (story):** Dean's affair exposed through the ring; Carla's quiet response (changing locks, calling her attorney) is the real payoff — no screaming, just precision.
- **Lock:** Carla Voss 41 (wife, cream turtleneck, reading glasses, holding a gift-wrap ribbon, expression shifting from confusion to cold clarity); "Helper" Jess Moreno 29 (holiday seasonal worker, red apron, dark hair in a clip, nervous smile, ring visible in apron pocket); Dean Voss 44 (husband, not present — at work). Setting: family living room, Naperville, IL, Wednesday 3pm, Christmas tree lit in the corner, wrapping station on the dining table, afternoon gray light through sheers. Object clue: gold wedding band resting on the kitchen counter beside a roll of tape.

### C31004 — F12-22 | Stepparent Given Last-Row Tickets → Donor Board Shows She Named the Scholarship
- **Setup:** At stepdaughter Kaylee's college graduation, bio-mom and family give stepmother Irene last-row tickets and exclude her from the family dinner reservation.

- **Payoff (story):** Kaylee realizes her stepmother quietly funded her entire education without ever asking for credit; the family's cruelty is exposed by the magnitude of Irene's generosity. Bittersweet — Irene got the back row despite paying for the whole show.
- **Lock:** Irene Park-Madsen 47 (stepmother, navy wrap dress, single strand of pearls, sitting alone in the last row, hands in lap, quiet tears); Kaylee Madsen 22 (graduate, cap and gown, stunned expression from stage); Bio-mom Trish Madsen 45 (front row, silk scarf, smug posture, clapping). Object clue: scholarship program booklet on Irene's lap (not readable).

### C31005 — F15-19 | Foster Teen Hired at a Diner → Customer Leaves Tip Note With Birth Mother's Phone Number
- **Setup:** Seventeen-year-old foster teen Dani Reeves gets her first job busing tables at a roadside diner outside Lexington, KY.
- **Clue:** A regular customer watches her closely for weeks; one day, the tip on the table is a folded note with a phone number and the words "I'm sorry. Call me."
- **Turn:** Dani calls the number — the voice on the other end is the woman who gave her up at two days old.
- **Payoff (story):** A tentative, imperfect reunion; the mother explains the circumstances (addiction, no support system); Dani doesn't forgive immediately but agrees to meet again. Mixed — longing fulfilled, but the gap of 17 years remains.
- **Lock:** Dani Reeves 17 (foster teen, diner uniform: white polo, black apron, hair in a bun, cautious eyes); Customer "Marie" Langford 35 (birth mother, denim jacket, fidgeting hands, sits in booth 4 every Thursday, dark circles under hopeful eyes); Diner owner Hank 60 (flannel, order pad, watching from behind the counter). Setting: Bluebird Roadside Diner, outside Lexington, KY, Thursday 6pm, neon sign glow through front window, red vinyl booths, Formica counter. Object clue: folded tip note left under a coffee mug (text not visible to camera).

### C31009 — F27-06 | Nightly Skype Calls From "The Desert" → IP Geolocation Shows Downtown Apartment
- **Setup:** Military spouse Tamika Rhodes endures months of grainy, static-filled Skype calls from her husband Dwayne, who claims to be deployed in the Middle East.

- **Payoff (story):** Tamika discovers Dwayne faked the deployment to live with another woman and collect "deployment" sympathy from the community; she confronts him not with rage but with the IP report and a packed bag. - **Lock:** Tamika Rhodes 30 (military spouse, USMC sweatshirt, hair in a silk wrap, laptop open on kitchen table, hand over mouth in shock); Dwayne Rhodes 32 (husband, not physically present — shown on Skype screen in camo T-shirt with artificial static); Friend's son Jaylen 16 (hoodie, showing Tamika his laptop with IP data). Object clue: Jaylen's laptop screen showing a map pin (no readable text, just a colored dot on a map).

### C31010 — F04-03 | Brother Finishes Glowing Eulogy → The Subject Clears His Throat in the Back Pew
- **Setup:** At a packed funeral in Portland, Maine, older brother Keith Garner delivers a moving eulogy for his younger brother Sean — lost at sea four months ago.
- **Clue:** The coast guard declared Sean presumed dead after his fishing boat was found empty, but no body was recovered.
- **Turn:** As Keith finishes the eulogy and the congregation bows their heads, a throat clears from the back pew. Sean is alive, standing in the doorway.
- **Payoff (story):** Sean survived; he was rescued by a cargo vessel and spent months in a foreign hospital with no ID. His return is joyful but fractured — the family collected insurance, sold his boat, and grieved genuinely. Reunion is messy, not clean.
- **Lock:** Keith Garner 42 (brother, black suit, red eyes, eulogy notes in shaking hand at podium); Sean Garner 38 (presumed dead, standing in church doorway, thinner, beard grown out, wearing borrowed clothes that don't fit, duffel bag at his feet); Mother Helen Garner 68 (front pew, black dress, tissue, turning toward the sound). Setting: St. Anne's Church, Portland, ME, Wednesday 11am, gray autumn light through tall windows, white flowers on a closed empty casket, full pews, organ pipes behind. Object clue: empty casket with flowers on top (Sean's body was never found).

## 4-beat arcs + Character Lock

### C53001 — F05-11 | Wife Attends Gala Solo → His Badge Scan Shows He Checked Into a Room
- **Setup:** Nadine Alford attends the annual corporate gala at a Philadelphia hotel alone because husband Grant claims he's at the conference keynote upstairs.
- **Clue:** Nadine notices the event coordinator's tablet shows badge scans — Grant's badge was scanned at the hotel room block, not the keynote hall.
- **Turn:** Nadine excuses herself, goes to the room floor, and finds his jacket hung outside a door that isn't their room.
- **Payoff:** Grant's assistant — the woman whose "performance review" required a private dinner — opens the door. Nadine photographs the room number, texts her attorney, and returns to the gala without a scene.
- **Lock:** Nadine Alford 36 (wife, emerald floor-length gown, hair in a French twist, clutch purse tight in both hands, jaw set); Grant Alford 39 (husband, absent from gala — referenced); Event coordinator Mia 28 (tablet in hand, lanyard). Setting: Hotel gala lobby with grand staircase, Philadelphia, PA, Saturday 9pm, chandelier light, cocktail crowd in formalwear. Object clue: event coordinator's tablet showing badge scan log.

### C53002 — F16-13 | Brother Calls Sister's Art "Hobby Trash" → Gallery Owner Arrives — Her Work Funds the Estate Taxes
- **Setup:** At a gallery reception in Santa Fe, brother Kent Villanueva mocks his sister Paloma's paintings displayed on the wall, calling them "hobby pieces."
- **Clue:** A woman in a linen blazer approaches with a sold-sticker sheet and speaks directly to Paloma.
- **Turn:** Gallery owner Renata Marsh announces Paloma's show sold out pre-opening — proceeds cover the family's delinquent estate taxes that Kent couldn't pay.
- **Payoff:** Kent realizes the "hobby" sister has been the financial backbone all along; Paloma's quiet talent speaks louder than his bluster.
- **Lock:** Paloma Villanueva 34 (sister/artist, paint-stained fingers, black linen jumpsuit, quiet pride); Kent Villanueva 40 (brother, sport coat over jeans, dismissive posture, wine glass); Gallery owner Renata Marsh 55 (linen blazer, sold-sticker sheet, warm authority). Setting: Gallery reception, Santa Fe, NM, Friday 7pm, adobe walls, track lighting, framed paintings. Object clue: sold-sticker sheet in Renata's hand.

### C53003 — F21-11 | Man Enters Pawn Shop to Retrieve His Father's Watch → He Now Owns the Shop
- **Setup:** Darius Webb walks into a pawn shop in Memphis to retrieve his late father's gold watch, pawned years ago during his gambling addiction.
- **Clue:** The pawnbroker doesn't recognize Darius but treats him dismissively, quoting triple the original pawn price.
- **Turn:** Darius sets down paperwork — he purchased the pawn shop's parent LLC last month. He owns the business, the inventory, and the watch.
- **Payoff:** Bittersweet — Darius reclaims the watch and the dignity, but the victory is hollow because his father never saw him recover. Grace over revenge: he keeps the staff employed.
- **Lock:** Darius Webb 37 (customer/secret owner, camel overcoat, close-cropped beard, LLC paperwork in an envelope, measured calm); Pawnbroker Lou Cassidy 60 (reading glasses on chain, flannel shirt, arms crossed, condescending smirk); Setting: Pawn shop counter, Memphis, TN, Tuesday 4pm, buzzing neon "OPEN" sign, glass display cases, fluorescent ceiling light. Object clue: LLC purchase documents in Darius's envelope.

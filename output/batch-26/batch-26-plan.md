## 4-beat arcs + Character Lock

### C26001 — F01-07 | Siblings Jeer at Old Camcorder Tape → Mother Disinherits the Bully on Screen
- **Setup:** Three adult siblings gather in a law office for the video will reading of their late mother. The eldest, Craig, has been gloating all week that he is named sole heir.
- **Clue:** The lawyer presses play on an old camcorder tape; the mother's face appears on a TV screen, calm and direct.
- **Turn:** On screen, the mother names her youngest daughter Naomi — the one Craig bullied out of the family — as sole heir. The diner, the house, everything.
- **Payoff (story):** Craig's cruelty loops back: the mother watched it all, documented it, and rewrote the will in her final months. Naomi inherits; Craig gets the camcorder.
- **Lock:** Craig Whitmore — 48, eldest son, navy sport coat too tight, red face, standing with arms half-raised in protest; Naomi Whitmore — 34, youngest daughter, olive cardigan, quiet hands, seated at the far end of the conference table; Attorney Lewis — 60, reading glasses, pressing play on a small TV mounted on a cart; Setting — Whitmore estate law office, Rittenhouse Square, Philadelphia, PA, Thursday 3 PM, dark wood paneling, green banker's lamp. Object clue: camcorder tape on the TV cart.

### C26002 — F05-18 | Daughter Opens Family Calendar → Recurring "Client Dinners" Map to One Apartment
- **Setup:** Sixteen-year-old Lily opens the shared family calendar on her iPad to check the week. Her father has "client dinner" every Thursday.
- **Clue:** All twelve "client dinner" entries over three months share a GPS pin — the same downtown apartment, not a restaurant.
- **Turn:** Lily shows her mother. The mother recognizes the address as belonging to a woman from the country club.
- **Payoff (story):** The father's own meticulously logged calendar becomes the evidence. The mother files. The karma is the precision of his own habits.
- **Lock:** Lily Ashford — 16, daughter, school uniform blazer, holding an iPad open to a calendar app; Claire Ashford — 42, mother, cream linen blouse, standing behind Lily at the kitchen island, face going pale; Setting — Ashford kitchen, Raleigh, NC, Sunday morning, granite island, coffee cups, morning light through plantation shutters. Object clue: iPad on the granite counter showing a calendar with repeating events.

### C26003 — F09-24 | "She's Seasonal Help — Ignore Her" → The Helper's Apron Pocket Holds a Matching Ring
- **Setup:** Husband Grant hires a "holiday helper" to wrap gifts in the den while wife Joanna is at work. The helper, Elena, is quiet and efficient.
- **Clue:** Joanna comes home early. Elena is gone but left her apron on the chair. In the pocket: a wedding band matching Grant's.
- **Turn:** Elena is not seasonal help. She is Grant's second wife from a marriage he never disclosed — a test arranged by Joanna's PI to get the ring in the same house.
- **Payoff (story):** The "nobody" (the PI's planted helper) had authority all along — Joanna set the trap. Grant is caught by his own invitation.
- **Lock:** Joanna Mercer — 39, wife, camel wool coat still on from walking in, one hand holding a plain gold band she found in the apron pocket; Grant Mercer — 41, husband, cable-knit sweater, caught mid-wrap at the den table, face draining; Elena (absent) — 30s, referenced only by the apron draped on a wingback chair. Setting — Mercer family den, Naperville, IL, Wednesday 5 PM, soft lamp light, gift-wrapping station on a large table, an apron on a wingback chair. Object clue: plain gold wedding band in Joanna's palm.

### C26004 — F10-04 | HR Insists the Meeting Was Not Recorded → Employee Shares the Cloud Link
- **Setup:** Software engineer Maya is told her position is eliminated "due to restructuring." HR manager Darren says the meeting was private and undocumented.
- **Clue:** The Zoom auto-record feature was enabled by IT policy. A cloud link exists.
- **Turn:** Maya shares the recording in which Darren admits the real reason: she filed a harassment complaint last month.
- **Payoff (story):** The company's own system captured the retaliation. Darren is terminated; Maya's complaint is reopened with teeth.
- **Lock:** Maya Redmond — 29, engineer, oversized flannel over a band tee, noise-canceling headphones around neck, sitting at a home desk with dual monitors; Darren Cole — 45, HR director, polo shirt with company logo, visible on one monitor in a Zoom window, smiling mid-denial; Setting — Maya's home office, Capitol Hill rowhouse, Seattle, WA, Tuesday 2 PM, afternoon rain visible through a window behind her monitors. Object clue: the small red "Recording" dot on the Zoom window that Darren doesn't notice.

### C26005 — F12-01 | Stepkids Banish New Spouse to the Sofa → Deed Shows the Spouse Bought the House
- **Setup:** Teenage stepchildren Aiden (17) and Bree (15) tape a note to the guest room door: "This is Mom's room. You sleep on the couch." New stepfather Marcus finds it his first night.
- **Clue:** Marcus does not argue. He goes to the kitchen and takes an envelope from his briefcase.
- **Turn:** The envelope contains the deed — Marcus bought this house six months before he married their mother. The previous owner was foreclosing; their mother could not afford it alone.
- **Payoff (story):** Full-circle role reversal: Marcus holds power but responds with grace. He tears up the note, tells the kids he will earn their trust, and sleeps on the couch anyway — by choice.
- **Lock:** Marcus Webb — 38, stepfather, gray henley, reading glasses pushed up on his forehead, holding a manila envelope in one hand and a taped note in the other; Aiden — 17, stepson, hoodie, arms crossed in the hallway; Bree — 15, stepdaughter, pajamas, peeking from behind Aiden; Setting — upstairs hallway of a split-level house, Columbus, OH, Friday 10 PM, warm hall light, family photos on the wall (none include Marcus). Object clue: the manila envelope (deed inside).

### C26006 — F15-01 | Foster Parents Prepare Adoption Papers → Doorbell Rings, Birth Mother Has a Court Order
- **Setup:** Sarah and James Lin sit at their dining table signing adoption finalization papers for Mia, the five-year-old they have fostered for three years.

- **Turn:** The woman is Mia's birth mother, Adrienne, with a court order halting the adoption — her parental rights have been reinstated after completing a two-year recovery program.
- **Payoff (story):** Bittersweet — Adrienne is genuinely recovered and legally right; Sarah and James lose Mia but cannot deny the child her mother. The truth arrives at the threshold too late for their dream but in time for the child's.
- **Lock:** Sarah Lin — 36, foster mother, soft gray sweater, adoption papers spread before her, pen in hand; James Lin — 38, foster father, flannel shirt, hand on Sarah's shoulder; Adrienne Cole — 28, birth mother, damp olive rain jacket, dark circles fading, holding a manila folder against her chest; Setting — Lin family front porch and dining room, shotgun house, Baton Rouge, LA, Saturday 4 PM, overcast gray light, screen door between them. Object clue: manila folder pressed to Adrienne's chest (court order inside).

### C26007 — F16-02 | Brother Changes Locks Before Memorial → Locksmith Receipt Shows Sister Paid
- **Setup:** After their father's death, brother Wade changes the lake house locks the day before the family memorial, blocking sister Colleen from entering.
- **Clue:** Colleen arrives with the original locksmith receipt — she paid for the lock installation years ago and retained a copy of the master key agreement.
- **Turn:** The locksmith's own records confirm Colleen holds the deed to the lake house; their father transferred it to her two years ago.
- **Payoff (story):** Wade's attempt to seize the property through intimidation backfires — the lock he paid to change still answers to a key his sister already holds.
- **Lock:** Wade Ellison — 46, brother, fleece vest over flannel, new padlock in hand, blocking the lake house door; Colleen Ellison-Park — 42, sister, rain jacket, holding a folded receipt and a brass key; Setting — lake house front porch, Lake Geneva, Wisconsin, Friday 6 PM, gray dusk, lake visible behind, wooden dock, autumn leaves. Object clue: brass key in Colleen's hand + folded receipt.

### C26008 — F23-05 | Housemates Vote to Evict Creator → Lease Is in the Evictee's Name

### C26009 — F26-01 | Officer Separates Couple for Questions → Wife Answers Perfectly, Husband Fails
- **Setup:** Married couple Davi and Nina sit in a USCIS field office for their Stokes interview — separate rooms, same questions. Nina (the beneficiary) knows every detail of their life.
- **Clue:** Through a thin wall, Nina hears Davi fumble a basic question — their pet's name, their bedsheet color. His voice sounds rehearsed and uncertain.
- **Turn:** Davi married Nina to help her stay. He does not love her the way she loves him. But the reason he fumbles is not fraud — it is that he has been sleeping at his mother's house for six months, protecting Nina from knowing he is sick.
- **Payoff (story):** The protector stayed away not from indifference but from sacrifice. The marriage is real in every way that matters — just not in the way the officer measures.
- **Lock:** Nina Santos — 30, wife/beneficiary, neat navy blouse, hands folded on the interview table, composed; Davi Santos — 33, husband/petitioner, white oxford slightly wrinkled, visible through a glass partition, rubbing his temple mid-question; Officer Tran — 50, USCIS examiner, gray suit, clipboard, seated across from Nina; Setting — USCIS Newark Field Office, New Jersey, Monday 9 AM, fluorescent light, government beige walls, partition glass between interview rooms. Object clue: the thin glass partition (Nina can see Davi struggling).

### C26010 — F27-01 | Town Prepares Homecoming Banner → Real Unit Roster Shows He Never Deployed
- **Setup:** Small-town Fayetteville prepares a hero's welcome for returning soldier Kyle Jessup. Banner across Main Street, flags, the whole community.
- **Clue:** Kyle's ex-girlfriend, now a recruiter at the local JROTC, runs the roster through a database. His name does not appear.
- **Turn:** The truth that exonerates: Kyle did enlist — but was medically discharged before deployment due to a heart condition he hid from everyone, including his family. He was too ashamed to come home without the uniform.
- **Payoff (story):** Kyle is not a fraud. He is a man who wanted to serve, was denied by his own body, and spent a year pretending because he could not face the town that celebrated him. The ex-girlfriend's investigation clears him of stolen valor but forces the truth: he needs help, not a parade.
- **Lock:** Kyle Jessup — 27, claimed soldier, desert camo jacket (real but worn pre-deployment discharge), thin face, standing beneath a banner looking up with a tight jaw; Rena Okafor — 28, ex-girlfriend/JROTC recruiter, olive field jacket, holding a phone with a database screen, standing twenty feet behind him; Setting — Main Street, Fayetteville, AR, Saturday 10 AM, a banner stretched between lampposts, American flags on every pole, small crowd gathering. Object clue: Rena's phone showing a search result (roster).

### C48001 | The Art Collection — Relatives Fight Over "Worthless" Paintings → Appraiser Finds Missing Master

**Seed:** F01-17 — Relatives fight over worthless paintings → the appraiser identifies one canvas as a missing master worth the entire estate

**Engine:** E00 — Humiliation → Reveal

**Arc:**
1. **Setup:** After patriarch Victor Hargrove's death, his three adult children gather at the estate attorney's office in Houston, TX for asset division. Eldest son Craig has already claimed the ranch, the cars, and the accounts. The only thing left for youngest daughter Simone is a stack of "amateur paintings" their father kept in the garage.
2. **Clue:** Craig and middle sister Darlene mock Simone as she loads canvases into her hatchback. One frame catches on the door — revealing a second canvas mounted behind it with unfamiliar brushwork.
3. **Turn:** Simone brings the paintings to a certified appraiser. The hidden canvas is a 1947 Franz Kline charcoal study, missing from a private collection since 1983. Authenticated value: $4.7 million — more than the rest of the estate combined.
4. **Payoff:** Simone's "worthless" inheritance eclipses everything Craig took. She funds an art scholarship in her father's name. Craig's lawyer calls begging to renegotiate. Simone declines.

**Character Lock:**
- SIMONE HARGROVE — 34, biracial (Black/white), natural curls pulled back with a clip, no makeup, oversized olive cardigan over a white tee, scuffed ankle boots; graphic designer, quiet, observant, the one her father actually talked to
- CRAIG HARGROVE — 41, white, receding blond hair, polo shirt tucked into khakis, Rolex; eldest son, aggressive, already claimed major assets
- DARLENE HARGROVE-KENT — 38, white, highlighted bob, cashmere wrap, arms crossed; middle child, sides with Craig
- Setting: Hargrove & Associates estate attorney conference room, Westheimer Rd, Houston, TX; Thursday 2 PM, overhead fluorescents, mahogany table
- Object clue: stack of framed canvases leaning against the conference room wall, one frame slightly ajar showing a second canvas behind

**Peak-tension (photo):** Craig pointing dismissively at the stack of paintings while Darlene laughs, Simone standing quietly near the canvases with one hand resting on a frame edge where a corner of the hidden canvas peeks out — Beat 2, twist NOT shown.

### C48002 | The Eulogy Interruption — Brother Finishes Glowing Eulogy → The "Deceased" Clears His Throat from the Back Pew

**Seed:** F04-03 — A brother finishes a glowing eulogy → the subject clears his throat in the back pew

**Engine:** E03 — Debt of Gratitude

**Arc:**
1. **Setup:** At Grace Lutheran Church in Duluth, MN, Karl Lindquist, 65, delivers a tearful eulogy for his younger brother Emil, 59, a carpenter who supposedly died in a boating accident on Lake Superior three weeks ago.
2. **Clue:** A man in a worn Carhartt jacket slips into the back pew during the eulogy. His hands are calloused, his beard unkempt — and he is listening to his own funeral with tears streaming.
3. **Turn:** Karl finishes: "Emil never got to know how much we needed him." A throat clears in the back row. Emil stands. "You can tell me now, Karl." The congregation turns. Emil faked nothing — he was rescued by a fishing trawler, hospitalized in Canada for three weeks with no ID, and just made it home.
4. **Payoff:** Emil's return reunites a family that had been fractured for years by a property dispute. Karl, who had refused to speak to Emil for five years over their father's cabin, realizes the eulogy he wrote was the apology he never gave while Emil was alive. They reconcile at the reception over coffee and lefse. The cabin goes to both of them.

**Character Lock:**
- KARL LINDQUIST — 65, white, silver crew cut, black suit that's slightly too tight, reading glasses, voice cracking at the podium; retired teacher, estranged from Emil over cabin inheritance
- EMIL LINDQUIST — 59, white, weathered face, unkempt gray-brown beard, Carhartt jacket over a hospital discharge shirt, bandaged left hand; carpenter, presumed drowned, rescued by Canadian trawler
- Setting: Grace Lutheran Church, Duluth, MN; Saturday 11 AM, winter, gray light through stained glass, pine pews, 60 mourners in dark coats
- Object clue: Emil's calloused hands gripping the back of the pew; hospital discharge band visible on wrist

**Peak-tension (photo):** Karl at the wooden podium mid-eulogy with tears on his cheeks, a framed photo of Emil on an easel beside the casket, and in the very back pew a figure in a Carhartt jacket barely visible among the dark coats, face partially hidden — Beat 2, twist NOT shown.

**Seed:** F09-06 — A volunteer visits daily while he works → the volunteer's car is registered to his offshore LLC

**Engine:** E12 — Karma / Comeuppance

**Arc:**
1. **Setup:** Nadine Carver, 36, notices a hospice volunteer named "Gwen" visiting her terminally ill mother-in-law at their Scottsdale, AZ home every weekday. Husband Elliott insists it's arranged through the agency. Nadine is grateful at first.
2. **Clue:** Nadine spots Gwen's silver Audi in the driveway — unusual for a volunteer. She photographs the plates. A DMV lookup shows the car registered to Cayman Bluffs LLC. The LLC's registered agent? Elliott Carver.
3. **Turn:** Nadine hires a PI. Gwen is not a hospice worker. She is Elliott's mistress, installed in their home under the cover of caring for his dying mother. Elliott has been funneling marital assets into Cayman Bluffs LLC — in Gwen's name — to hide them before a divorce he's been planning.
4. **Payoff:** Nadine's attorney files for divorce first, with full asset tracing. The offshore LLC is disclosed to the court and frozen. Elliott loses the house (community property state). His mother, still lucid, changes her will to leave everything to Nadine — the only one who actually cared.

- ELLIOTT CARVER — 39, white, dark hair slicked back, tailored navy blazer over a white crew-neck, smart watch; financial consultant, claims long hours, building an exit plan

### C48006 | The Foster Teen Job — Teen Hired at a Diner → Customer Leaves a Tip Note with Birth Mother's Phone Number

**Seed:** F15-19 — Foster teen hired at a diner → customer leaves tip note — birth mother's phone number

**Engine:** E11 — Bittersweet / Too Late

**Arc:**
1. **Setup:** Cleo Rawlings, 16, foster kid on her third placement, lands her first job bussing tables at the Pinewood Diner on Route 8 outside Erie, PA. She is quiet, fast, invisible — the way she's learned to survive.
2. **Clue:** A woman in her forties sits alone every Tuesday, orders only coffee, watches Cleo work. She always leaves an oversized tip with a folded napkin underneath. This week, the napkin has a phone number and the words: "When you're ready. — Donna."
3. **Turn:** Cleo's foster mother recognizes the name: Donna Rawlings, Cleo's birth mother, released from a women's facility six months ago. Donna has been coming every week, too afraid to speak, leaving bread-crumb notes hoping Cleo will choose.
4. **Payoff:** Cleo calls the number. They meet in the parking lot. Donna does not hug her — she waits. "I'm not asking to come back," Donna says. "I'm just asking if I can sit at the counter while you work." Cleo nods. It is not a reunion. It is a beginning — tentative, fragile, and sixteen years too late. Bittersweet because the years can't be returned, but the door is open.

**Character Lock:**

- DONNA RAWLINGS — 42, white, thin, weathered face softened by sobriety, brown hair tucked behind ears, denim jacket, plain silver ring; birth mother, released 6 months ago, afraid to approach

- Object clue: folded napkin with a phone number and "When you're ready. — Donna" tucked under a coffee mug

**Engine:** E27 — The Protector Who Stayed Away

**Arc:**

2. **Clue:** Senior MP Sergeant Diaz notices: PO2 Callister lost his left ring finger in a shipboard accident two years ago. The man at the gate has all ten fingers. He is not Ryan. He is Ryan's identical twin, Marcus.

Marcus is charged with a misdemeanor but the CO quietly drops it after learning the circumstances. The brothers reunite in a waiting room. Marcus says: "I stayed away because you told me to. I'm not staying away again." Bittersweet — the system failed, the brother broke it, and the cost of "protecting from a distance" almost came too late.

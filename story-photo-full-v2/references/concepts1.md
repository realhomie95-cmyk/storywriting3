# Concepts 1 — Category Bank (A–M)

13 category groups of drama scenarios for story-photo-full-v1 (4:5 photo + caption + full story).
All scenarios are set in the United States.

---

## Category A — Rich vs Poor (Giàu và Nghèo)

Contrast between wealth/status and the appearance of poverty. The twist always involves the "poor" person having hidden significance, connection, or power.

### Core twist types in this category
| Twist | Setup |
|---|---|
| Hidden benefactor | Person being dismissed funded something the dismisser depends on |
| Lost family | Object (ring, photo, necklace) triggers recognition of family connection |
| True creator | "Poor" person is the actual designer/artist/founder behind the brand |
| Community representative | Individual carries the weight and hope of an entire community |
| Self-sacrifice | Person was secretly paying for something that helped the dismisser |

### High-performing scenario templates
1. Bridal shop humiliation → owner recognizes heirloom ring
2. Fine dining turns away old man → businessman recognizes him as his shelter benefactor
3. Poor boy rejected at private school → his essay funds the new science wing
4. Apartment lobby insults market woman → she donated the land for the school
5. Jewelry shop mocks broken necklace → stone belongs to the family that owns the mall
6. Waitress returns wallet → she's the missing daughter in the photograph
7. Poor fiancé humiliated at engagement party → he secretly saved the family company
8. Hospital VIP room removes old woman → doctor runs over: she funded the pediatric floor
9. Luxury boutique dismisses old man buying scarf → his hospitalized wife designed the first three collections
10. Real estate agent laughs at coins → 43 families pooling savings to buy back their community home

## Mixing Scenarios (Hybrid Prompts)

For "MIX" batches, combine 2–3 scenarios from different categories. The best combinations create layered twists where the surface conflict is Category A or B, but the emotional core is Category C (military/sacrifice always adds depth).

### Effective combination patterns

| Mix Type | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| A + C | Poor/dismissed person is a veteran | Market woman who donated land → also funded veterans' shelter wing |
| B + C | Workplace test → military connection | Security guard test → intern's act of kindness was being recorded because her grandmother served |
| A + B | Hidden wealth + moral test | Disguised billionaire + the one employee who helped was already being observed |
| B + B | Two twist types stack | Wrongful accusation cleared by audio evidence from a person who is themselves the hidden founder |
| A + C + B | Full triple mix | Rich/poor setup + forgotten veteran + workplace karma all in 15 seconds |

### Mix rules
1. Each mix needs **one primary setup** (the humiliation/dismissal) and **one primary twist** (the reveal)
2. Secondary scenario elements add texture — they should not require explanation
3. The twist must still land in the 11-15s segment — don't overcrowd
4. Animal/child recognition works as a third layer in any A+C or B+C combo

### Avoiding repetition across a batch

Across 30 prompts, vary:
- **Location**: Arena, hospital, fine dining, car showroom, military base, art gallery, bakery, graduation, courtroom, park, boutique, boardroom — don't use same location more than 2–3 times
- **Protagonist type**: Old man, old woman, teenage girl, young employee, delivery worker, soldier, janitor, child — rotate
- **Twist mechanism**: Photo, ring, audio recording, dog tags, scar, animal recognition, child recognition, envelope, uniform, shoes — rotate
- **Humiliation actor**: Manager, security guard, wealthy customer, police officer, interviewer, auctioneer — rotate

## US Setting Conventions

### Dialogue style
All English dialogue should feel natural and American:
- Informal dismissals: "You can't be in here", "Let's go", "Sir, I need you to leave"
- Formal dismissals: "This area is restricted", "This is a reservation-only establishment"
- Emotional reveals: Don't over-script — one strong line beats three sentimental ones

### Location specificity
Use real-sounding but specific American locations:
- Not: "a luxury restaurant" → Yes: "a reservation-only Manhattan restaurant, night, mixed warm and cool lighting"
- Not: "a hospital" → Yes: "a private hospital VIP waiting room, warm white lighting, polished marble"
- Not: "a school" → Yes: "an East Coast private middle school corridor, autumn afternoon, natural light"

### Military references
- Use real branch names: US Army, US Marine Corps, Special Forces, Navy SEAL
- Use real decorations: Medal of Honor, Purple Heart, Distinguished Service Medal
- Use real terminology: dog tags, unit patch, service record, field hospital, deployment
- Avoid inventing fake unit names or medals

### Character naming
- When a name is needed, use common American names: James, Marcus, Roy, Eleanor, Helen
- Names add realism but are optional — most prompts work without them
- Avoid names that sound like stereotypes or are culturally mismatched to the US setting

## Category D — Family Secrets & Reunions

Hidden family connections, long-lost relatives, adoption truths, and DNA reveals. The twist is always relational — the dismisser and the dismissed are bound by blood, history, or sacrifice they don't yet know about.

### Core twist types in this category
| Twist | Setup |
|---|---|
| Lost child/parent | Object (photo, birthmark, locket) triggers recognition of family member thought dead or gone |
| Adoption reveal | Person finds out the stranger they mistreated raised them, or is their biological parent |
| Secret sibling | Two strangers share the same parent — one rich, one not |
| Hidden lineage | Person being dismissed is the biological heir to something the dismisser controls |
| Sacrifice debt | Parent gave up everything (kidney, savings, custody) for child who doesn't know |

### High-performing scenario templates
1. DNA test clinic receptionist dismisses walk-in → walk-in matches the receptionist's biological father
2. Social worker condescends to single mother → she later discovers she was adopted by that woman's family as a baby
3. Wealthy family rejects son's girlfriend → grandmother recognizes the girl's locket as her daughter's — the one who died giving birth
4. Hospital nurse is cold to elderly patient → charge nurse runs over: "That's the man who donated his kidney so your brother could live"
5. Adoption attorney dismisses nervous woman's paperwork → paralegal recognizes her name from a sealed record: she's looking for the child she gave up, not a child to adopt
6. Teacher mocks child for not knowing her father → school counselor pulls her aside: the janitor who waves to her every morning has been paying her lunch account for three years
7. Funeral director is impatient with a homeless man → the man reaches into his coat and places a folded letter on the casket — written in the deceased's handwriting, addressed to him
8. Bride's family humiliates groom at rehearsal dinner → groom's grandmother walks in and the bride's mother goes pale: they are half-sisters
9. Real estate agent dismisses old man as a squatter → title search reveals his name is on the deed, placed there by a daughter the agent never knew his father had
10. College admissions officer rejects an application → the rejected student's file contains a letter of recommendation signed by the officer's own estranged father

## Category E — Medical & Hospital Drama

Life-or-death settings where hidden sacrifices, medical histories, and identity reveals carry maximum emotional weight. The stakes are already high — the twist makes them personal.

### Core twist types in this category
| Twist | Setup |
|---|---|
| The doctor who saved you | Person belittling the nurse/doctor is alive because of that exact person's hands |
| Hidden patient history | The "nobody" being dismissed has a medical file that changes everything |
| Organ donor connection | Person meets — without knowing — the one who received or gave a life-saving organ |
| Bedside sacrifice | Someone stayed with a patient for days when the family didn't — family finds out in the worst way |
| Misdiagnosis justice | Patient dismissed as anxious or poor later revealed to be right all along, often saving others |

### High-performing scenario templates
1. Hospital administrator demotes senior nurse → new board member walks in: "She held my chest closed in a field hospital. Don't touch her."
2. Wealthy patient berates ICU orderly for being slow → doctor pulls up the orderly's file: he donated a lobe of his liver to a child in this ward two years ago
3. ER receptionist turns away man without insurance → charge nurse intervenes: "He's the one who carried Dr. Reyes out of a car fire in 2018"
4. Pediatric surgeon dismisses older colleague's suggestion → old colleague quietly says: "I performed your first surgery. You were four. Your mother couldn't afford it."
5. Family demands better care than the "common patients" → nurse turns: "The man in room 6 donated the wing you're standing in. His name is above the door."
6. Medical student mocks attending physician's old-fashioned methods → resident pulls her aside: "His method is in the textbook. He wrote it."
7. Insurance adjuster rejects transplant coverage → patient's daughter stands up: "She donated her kidney to your CEO's son three years ago. Pull the records."
8. Arrogant specialist dismisses hospice nurse's concern → patient opens his eyes and says the nurse's name — he's the specialist's father, who left when he was six
9. Hospital security stops "vagrant" from entering → ICU attending runs out: "That's the paramedic who kept my patient alive for 40 minutes on the side of a highway. Let him in."
10. Young doctor ridicules elderly patient's home remedy → pharmacist at the station looks up: "That compound is in the FDA trial she funded. She's the lead researcher."

## Category F — Education & Mentorship

Schools, universities, tutoring centers, and graduation stages where teachers, students, and administrators misjudge each other. Twist always involves hidden academic legacy, sacrifice, or hidden brilliance.

### Core twist types in this category
| Twist | Setup |
|---|---|
| Hidden scholar | Person dismissed as uneducated turns out to have written the curriculum being used |
| Funding reversal | Student or family being rejected is the anonymous source of the scholarship they're applying for |
| Teacher's origin | Arrogant educator is confronted with their own humble beginning by someone who was there |
| The student who came back | Former dropout returns in a position of power to face the teacher who gave up on them |
| Silent benefactor | Janitor, cafeteria worker, or bus driver was secretly putting kids through school |

### High-performing scenario templates
1. Admissions dean rejects applicant for poor grades → provost runs in: "He wrote the algorithm that runs our entire enrollment system. He's 19."
2. English teacher mocks ESL student's accent → another teacher stops her: "Her mother was a linguistics professor at Oxford before they fled"
3. Principal cancels janitor's retirement party ("budget") → school board chair stands up: "He paid for 14 students' graduation fees out of pocket over 22 years"
4. University professor dismisses mature student's thesis → department head enters: "That argument was published in 1987. She wrote it."
5. Financial aid officer denies scholarship extension → scholarship director walks in: "The endowment funding this scholarship was established by her late husband. His name is on the wall behind you."
6. Coach cuts high school player from team → scout in the bleachers walks down: "I drove three hours to watch him. You just cut the fastest 40-time I've recorded in a decade."
7. Graduate school rejects applicant for lacking publications → admissions committee member looks up: "She published under a pen name. I assigned her paper in my seminar last spring."
8. School librarian dismisses boy for "just looking at pictures" → child holds up the book: he's been reading it — it's in Mandarin, his first language
9. PTA president humiliates working mother for missing meetings → principal intervenes: "She built our reading program from scratch. Every book in this library came from her grant."
10. Professor fails student for "plagiarism" → dean of students enters with a document: "The original paper was written by her father. He donated it to the public domain before he died. She cited herself."

## Category G — Sports & Athletics

Stadiums, gyms, locker rooms, and training facilities. Hidden coaches, forgotten athletes, stolen records, and the people who made champions without ever getting credit.

### Core twist types in this category
| Twist | Setup |
|---|---|
| The maker behind the star | Person dismissed trained, coached, or sacrificed for the athlete being celebrated |
| Stolen record | Achievement being honored belongs to someone who was erased from the record books |
| Retired legend unrecognized | Former champion in plain clothes dismissed by those who owe their career to them |
| Equipment room hero | The invisible person (equipment manager, waterboy, trainer) holds the real story |
| The scout who saw first | Person dismissed as nobody was the one who found the star before anyone else did |

### High-performing scenario templates
1. Stadium security removes old man from courtside → starting point guard stops warm-up: "He coached me in a church gym when I had nowhere to go. He stays."
2. Athletic director fires aging trainer → three players stand up simultaneously in the locker room — he rehabbed all three of them from career-ending injuries
3. Sports journalist dismisses "washed-up" former player at press event → the league's all-time scoring record belongs to the man she just dismissed — it was set under a name he no longer uses
4. High school coach cuts the smallest kid at tryouts → opposing team's head coach walks over: "I started my career getting cut too. Tell him I'll be watching him."
5. Front office dismisses team chaplain's contract → quarterback stands up: "I called him at 2 AM the night before the championship. He's the reason I played."
6. Gym owner kicks out old man for using equipment slowly → member tells him: "He built this gym. His name is on the original deed. You bought it from his son."
7. Boxing promoter mocks retired corner man → champion removes his belt and hands it to the old man: "He's the one who taught me to fight. The belt belongs to him first."
8. College scout dismisses walk-on player → equipment manager steps forward: "I've been logging his times for two months. He's faster than your first-round pick."
9. Sports awards host skips over a name → old woman in the back row stands: "That was my husband's record. He died before the association reinstated it. His name should be read."
10. Franchise owner refuses to honor retired number → team's oldest living player stands at center court: "That number belonged to the man who integrated this league. You will honor it."

## Category H — Legal & Justice

Courtrooms, law offices, parole hearings, and police stations. Wrongful convictions, last-minute testimony, evidence that surfaces at the worst possible moment, and justice arriving years late.

### Core twist types in this category
| Twist | Setup |
|---|---|
| The innocent bystander | Person convicted or blamed had nothing to do with it — someone in the room knows |
| Late evidence | A document, recording, or witness surfaces that overturns a narrative everyone accepted |
| The victim in the room | Person of power is confronted by the person their decision destroyed |
| Witness who was there | Unremarkable person (janitor, parking attendant, café worker) saw everything |
| Career built on injustice | Public official's reputation collapses when the person they wronged walks into the ceremony |

### High-performing scenario templates
1. DA's retirement ceremony interrupted → woman stands: "My brother served eleven years for a crime your office knew he didn't commit. I have the memo."
2. Judge dismisses public defender's motion without reading it → court reporter quietly says: "Your Honor, that's the same argument that overturned the Martinez conviction last month. I transcribed both."
3. Parole board denies release without reviewing new evidence → prisoner's daughter holds up a phone: "The actual perpetrator confessed on video six months ago. Your office received it."
4. Lawyer dismisses elderly witness as confused → witness opens her purse and places a photograph on the table — it shows the defendant's alibi, timestamped and signed
5. Sheriff celebrates conviction at town hall → woman at the back: "You arrested the wrong man. My son has been in a cell for four years. The right person is in this room."
6. Law school honors alumnus for landmark case → the man the case was built against stands up: "I lost my house, my business, and my marriage. The key witness recanted. Ask him why that didn't make the news."
7. Attorney general dismisses small-town legal aid lawyer → the case file she's carrying just overturned three convictions in the district he oversees
8. Court security stops man from entering sentencing hearing → judge looks up from the bench: "That's the victim's father. He has a right to be heard. Let him in."
9. Prosecutor mocks defendant's character witness as irrelevant → defense reveals the witness is an undercover federal agent who spent eight months on the case — and the defendant didn't know
10. Newly appointed police commissioner gives speech about integrity → woman in the audience holds up a folder: "Page one is a report your predecessor buried. Your name is on the cover letter."

## Category I — Food & Culinary

Restaurants, bakeries, food trucks, and kitchen competitions. Recipe theft, culinary legacy, food memory triggers, and the invisible labor behind celebrated cuisine.

### Core twist types in this category
| Twist | Setup |
|---|---|
| Stolen recipe | The dish being celebrated belongs to someone in the room who was never credited |
| Food memory trigger | A taste or smell unlocks a buried connection between dismisser and dismissed |
| The invisible cook | Dishwasher, prep cook, or line worker holds the real culinary knowledge in the building |
| Family restaurant legacy | "Nobody" is the founder's family — the restaurant literally wouldn't exist without them |
| Critic's blind spot | Food critic dismisses dish that turns out to be the one they've spent years searching for |

### High-performing scenario templates
1. Celebrity chef dismisses line cook's technique in front of cameras → sous chef steps forward: "That technique is in your cookbook, Chef. On page 47. She taught it to you."
2. Michelin inspector is curt with elderly busboy → executive chef runs from kitchen: "Sir, please. He is the reason this restaurant exists. My grandfather learned to cook from him."
3. Restaurant owner fires longtime dishwasher for being "too slow" → health inspector walks in: "He flagged a contamination issue three years ago that kept you from losing your license. It's in the record."
4. Food competition judge dismisses home cook's dish as "unsophisticated" → another judge tastes it and goes quiet: "This is the exact dish I had in a village outside Oaxaca twenty years ago. Where did you learn this?"
5. Catering manager fires immigrant kitchen worker for English → banquet chef stops her: "She has a culinary degree. She trained under Pierre Gagnaire in Paris. She's been teaching my team for six months."
6. Breakfast diner owner humiliates elderly customer for being slow to order → longtime regular leans over: "He invented the waffle iron your whole menu runs on. The patent made your grandfather's first location possible."
7. Cooking show host dismisses contestant's "simple" recipe → network producer enters the studio: "That recipe has 4.2 million saves on the platform. It's the most-shared dish in show history. And she submitted it two years ago — you rejected it."
8. New restaurant owner fires the cook who came with the building → health rating drops immediately; former owner calls: "She's the only one who knows the source suppliers, the ratios, and how to manage that oven. Rehire her today."
9. Food critic writes scathing review, then meets the chef → chef is a 70-year-old Vietnamese woman who doesn't speak English; her daughter translates the review — and then hands the critic a letter from the James Beard Foundation naming her a regional legend
10. Soup kitchen director dismisses volunteer as "not trained" → hospital nutritionist on staff: "She designed the dietary program for three county shelters. I consulted her when I wrote ours."

## Category J — Romance & Relationships

Love tests, hidden devotion, relationship betrayals, and second chances. Twist reveals that what appeared to be manipulation or coldness was actually profound loyalty, sacrifice, or restraint.

### Core twist types in this category
| Twist | Setup |
|---|---|
| The love test that backfired | Person who faked poverty/illness/failure to test their partner finds the test revealed something about themselves |
| Decade-long devotion | Person who seemed absent was protecting, funding, or waiting in ways the other never knew |
| The one who walked away first | Person returning to a relationship learns the other's silence was a choice that cost them everything |
| Proof of commitment | "Small" gesture (a ring kept, a letter unsent, a seat saved) reveals the depth of feeling |
| The wrong conclusion | Misunderstanding that drove them apart is explained in the worst possible moment — too late or just in time |

### High-performing scenario templates
1. Woman breaks up with man for "not being ambitious" → his business partner walks in: "He turned down the acquisition offer because the company HQ would have moved you away from your mother's care"
2. Man confronts girlfriend for lying about her job → she was working night shifts to pay off his student debt without telling him — her pay stubs are in the drawer he's pulling open
3. Rich woman tests boyfriend by pretending to lose everything → he passes every test but breaks up with her when she confesses: "I don't want to be with someone who needed to test me to feel safe"
4. Woman at divorce proceeding demands to know why husband stopped trying → his attorney places a folder on the table: eighteen months of medical records showing he was in treatment and didn't want her to cancel her career opportunity to care for him
5. Man coldly returns engagement ring at restaurant → woman notices his hands are shaking; her sister slides a note across: "He found out about his diagnosis two days ago. He didn't want you to stay out of pity."
6. Girlfriend accuses boyfriend of forgetting their anniversary → he hands her a receipt: the reservation was made six months ago, deposit paid, flowers ordered — all canceled the day she told him her mother was in the hospital
7. Woman at airport, certain she's being left → man running through the terminal isn't leaving — he's arriving, two years early, having finished everything faster to get back to her
8. Husband discovers wife's secret savings account → she's been saving since year one — not to leave, but for the house she heard him describe once, on their third date, when he thought she wasn't listening
9. Woman confronts ex who "moved on too fast" → mutual friend quietly says: "He hasn't moved on. That's his cousin. He's been sitting in his car outside your apartment three nights a week to make sure the lights go on."
10. Man ends relationship because "she deserves better" → she hands him a letter she wrote and never sent, dated three years ago: "I knew. I stayed anyway. Ending it doesn't make you noble. It just makes you gone."

## Category K — Immigration & Cultural Identity

First and second-generation immigrant stories, naturalization ceremonies, language barriers, and the hidden status of people dismissed for not "belonging." Twist always reframes the dismissal as a profound misreading of who the person actually is.

### Core twist types in this category
| Twist | Setup |
|---|---|
| Hidden status or credential | Person dismissed for poor English holds a degree, medal, or position of international significance |
| The founding generation | "Foreign-looking" person dismissed in a space that exists because of their community's sacrifice |
| Language as armor | Person who appeared not to understand English understood everything — and recorded it |
| The child's translation | Child interprets for parent, then reveals something the parent asked them not to say |
| Citizenship ceremony reveal | Moment of naturalization intersects with a power reversal involving someone who doubted them |

### High-performing scenario templates
1. Airport customs officer is condescending to elderly Asian woman → she opens her passport: she is a sitting judge in the country the officer just cited as a "risky origin"
2. Restaurant manager fires immigrant dishwasher for "communication issues" → health inspector walks in speaking Mandarin — he responds fluently, then switches to English: "I hold a food safety certification your manager has never passed"
3. Landlord lectures immigrant tenant on "how things work in America" → tenant holds up naturalization certificate dated 1987: "I've been American longer than you've owned this building"
4. DMV clerk is dismissive of woman who can't read English form → her daughter translates, then adds quietly: "She is a physician. She delivered babies in three countries. She will learn English when she has time."
5. City council member argues against immigrant community center → elderly man at the back of the room stands: "My father built the first community center in this district in 1948. With his own hands. Your office used it for forty years. I have the deed."
6. HR manager rejects resume for "foreign-sounding name" → recruiter at the next desk pulls up the portfolio: the applicant designed the product the company's entire revenue line is built on, published under a pen name
7. School principal dismisses concerned immigrant parent as not understanding the system → parent places a document on the desk: she was a school principal for fourteen years before she came here
8. Grocery store manager accuses immigrant worker of not understanding instructions → colleague pulls him aside: "He has a master's degree in logistics. He was running warehouse operations for 200 people six months ago. He understood the instructions. He was correcting them."
9. Naturalization ceremony official is perfunctory, almost rude → the senator present stands up mid-ceremony: "I want to acknowledge this man specifically. He saved eleven Americans during an evacuation in 2004. By name. I was one of them."
10. Tech startup investor dismisses pitch from immigrant founder for "cultural fit" → associate leans over: "His last company was acquired for $340 million. He took the check in citizenship paperwork he was still waiting on. He's here because he believes in this market."

## Category L — Art, Music & Creative Industry

Concert halls, recording studios, galleries, film sets, and creative agencies. Stolen credit, misattributed work, session musicians who made the hit, and the invisible creatives behind celebrated names.

### Core twist types in this category
| Twist | Setup |
|---|---|
| Ghost creator | The actual creator of a celebrated work is in the room and unknown to the people praising it |
| Stolen attribution | Work being honored under one name belongs to someone who was deliberately erased |
| The session ghost | Musician/artist whose uncredited contribution defined the work is dismissed as irrelevant |
| Early supporter | Person dismissed once believed in the celebrated artist when no one else would |
| The original | Celebrated version is a copy; the original is standing at the door, or sitting in the corner |

### High-performing scenario templates
1. Gallery director is dismissive of old woman asking questions about a painting → research curator walks over: "She is the painter. The attribution was corrected last year. Her name should be on the wall."
2. Record label VP dismisses aging session guitarist at industry event → the hit playing in the background uses his guitar riff — uncredited, from a 1994 recording session he was paid $200 for
3. Film award ceremony credits director for original score → orchestra conductor in the front row has the original handwritten sheet music in his lap — with a different name at the top
4. Art school dean rejects applicant's portfolio as "derivative" → visiting professor looks up: "This technique was pioneered by one painter. She is the only living person who uses it this way. Who taught you?"
5. Music producer dismisses songwriter's demo → A&R rep enters: "That melody is in the demo you submitted to us three years ago. We passed. It's now on a track with 800 million streams. We owe her a conversation."
6. Theater director fires aging stage manager for being "past it" → leading actor refuses to go on: "She has blocked every show in this building for thirty years. She knows where every cable is. I don't go on without her."
7. Photography agency rejects portfolio as "too old-fashioned" → editor at the table recognizes one image: "This photograph won a World Press Photo award in 1991. It changed how my generation thought about documentary work. Who are you?"
8. Podcast host dismisses technical producer as "just the sound guy" → network executive calls mid-show: "The production quality on your show is why we signed you. The sound guy built that from scratch."
9. Creative director steals junior designer's concept and presents it as their own → client's in-house designer holds up a screenshot: "She posted this concept in our shared Slack channel fourteen months ago. I saved it because I loved it."
10. Literary agent dismisses manuscript as "not commercial" → publisher at the same conference holds up a printed copy: "We acquired this last week. Foreign rights sold in eleven countries in 48 hours. I'd look more carefully next time."

## Category M — Community, Faith & Local Heroes

Churches, food banks, community centers, neighborhood meetings, and small-town institutions. Anonymous donors, forgotten founders, community heroes dismissed by those they built everything for.

### Core twist types in this category
| Twist | Setup |
|---|---|
| Anonymous founder | The person being dismissed built, funded, or organized the very institution dismissing them |
| Neighborhood memory | Long-term resident holds the institutional memory that saves a community — and is treated like a burden |
| Crisis hero | Person dismissed as unremarkable was the one who held the community together during its hardest moment |
| Silent tithe | Individual who appears to have nothing has been giving consistently and significantly for years |
| The first one | Person dismissed is the first member, first donor, or first volunteer — without whom none of this exists |
